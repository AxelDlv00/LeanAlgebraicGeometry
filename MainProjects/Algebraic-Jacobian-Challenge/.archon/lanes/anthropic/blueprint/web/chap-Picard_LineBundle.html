3 Line bundles on schemes and the Picard group

This chapter sets up the foundational Phase B/C infrastructure: the type of line bundles (invertible quasi-coherent sheaves) on a scheme \(X\), the abelian-group structure under tensor product, and the pull-back functoriality. It is the smallest self-contained step toward the Picard scheme construction (Theorem 7) and feeds Steps 2–6 of the Jacobian gap chain.

The chapter introduces no protected declarations: every definition and lemma here is auxiliary, supporting the construction of \(\Pic _{C/k}\) in later phases.

3.1 Definition

Definition 3 Line bundle on a scheme
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.LineBundle

Let \(X\) be a scheme. A line bundle on \(X\) is an invertible quasi-coherent \(\mathcal O_X\)-module: a sheaf \(L \in X.\mathtt{Modules}\) that is quasi-coherent and locally free of rank \(1\). Equivalently (Mathlib formulation): an object of \(X.\mathtt{Modules}\) that is invertible with respect to the tensor product \(- \otimes _{\mathcal O_X} -\) in the symmetric monoidal category \(X.\mathtt{Modules}\).

Mathlib has AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.Modules (sheaves of \(\mathcal O_X\)-modules) and the SheafOfModules.QuasicoherentData typeclass for quasi-coherence. The combination "invertible quasi-coherent sheaf" is not pre-packaged; it is the content of this definition.

3.2 Group structure under tensor product

Theorem 4 Picard group of a scheme
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.instCommGroupLineBundle

For every scheme \(X\), the type of line bundles on \(X\) (modulo isomorphism) carries a canonical commutative-group structure. The operation is tensor product over \(\mathcal O_X\), the unit is the structure sheaf \(\mathcal O_X\) itself, and the inverse of a line bundle \(L\) is its \(\mathcal O_X\)-dual \(L^\vee = \mathcal{H}om_{\mathcal O_X}(L, \mathcal O_X)\).

We denote this group by \(\Pic (X)\) and call it the Picard group of \(X\).

Proof ▶

Tensor product of two invertible quasi-coherent sheaves is invertible quasi-coherent (locally on \(X\) this reduces to the corresponding statement for invertible modules over commutative rings, which Mathlib has via Module.Invertible). The structure sheaf is the tensor unit. The dual sheaf is the inverse: \(L \otimes _{\mathcal O_X} L^\vee \cong \mathcal O_X\) canonically. Commutativity and associativity descend from the symmetric monoidal structure on \(X.\mathtt{Modules}\).

3.3 Pull-back functoriality

Theorem 5 Pull-back of line bundles
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.Pic.pullback

Let \(f \colon X \to Y\) be a morphism of schemes. Pull-back of \(\mathcal O_Y\)-modules along \(f\) restricts to a group homomorphism

\[ f^* \colon \Pic (Y) \; \to \; \Pic (X),\qquad L \; \mapsto \; f^* L \; :=\; f^{-1} L \otimes _{f^{-1}\mathcal O_Y} \mathcal O_X. \]

Pull-back preserves invertibility, commutes with tensor product, and is functorial: \((\mathrm{id}_X)^* = \mathrm{id}_{\Pic (X)}\) and \((f \circ g)^* = g^* \circ f^*\).

Proof ▶

Mathlib has ModuleCat.tensorObj for the tensor product of \(\mathcal O_X\)-modules and the pullback of sheaves of modules along a scheme morphism. Pull-back commutes with tensor product (Mathlib/CategoryTheory/Monoidal/Functor.lean for the abstract statement; the geometric instance is the content of the theorem). Functoriality is the standard sheaf-pullback functoriality.

3.4 Use in the project

The Picard group \(\Pic (X)\) is the underlying set (modulo isomorphism) of the Picard scheme \(\Pic _{C/k}\) in the curve case; the scheme structure adds a \(k\)-scheme of finite type representing the relative Picard functor \(S \mapsto \Pic (C \times _k S) / \Pic (S)\).

This chapter is therefore the gateway to:

  • Definition 6 (Picard functor of \(C/k\)) — quotient by \(\Pic (S)\) uses Theorem 5.

  • Theorem 7 (representability of the Picard scheme) — uses the full \(\Pic \) machinery built here.

  • Definition 14 (Abel–Jacobi map) — defined via the line bundle \(\mathcal O_{C \times C}(\Delta - p_1^* P)\) on \(C \times C\), an explicit element of \(\Pic (C \times C)\).

3.5 Mathlib gap

Mathlib b80f227 has:

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.Modules — the abelian category of sheaves of \(\mathcal O_X\)-modules on a scheme \(X\).

  • SheafOfModules.QuasicoherentData — quasi-coherence as data on a sheaf of modules.

  • Module.Invertible — invertibility for modules over a commutative ring.

  • Mathlib/RingTheory/PicardGroup.lean — the Picard group of a commutative ring (CommRing.Pic R).

What is missing and supplied by this chapter:

  • A scheme-side type of invertible quasi-coherent sheaves and the proof that this property is preserved by tensor product.

  • A scheme-side CommGroup instance on the Picard group.

  • A scheme-morphism-functorial pull-back homomorphism \(\Pic (Y) \to \Pic (X)\).

These are mapped to Phase B/C step 1 of STRATEGY.md.
