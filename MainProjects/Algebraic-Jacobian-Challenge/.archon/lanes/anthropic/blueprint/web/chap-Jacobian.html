5 The Jacobian as an abelian variety

Let \(C\) be a smooth proper curve over \(k\) in the sense of Chapter 2, with genus \(g = g(C)\).

The Picard scheme machinery underlying the construction of \(\Jac (C)\) — in particular the relative Picard functor (Definition 6) and its bundled representability theorem (Theorem 7) — is set up in Chapter 4. This chapter consumes those declarations to define the Jacobian and prove the four abelian-variety instances.

5.1 The Jacobian

Definition 8 Jacobian
✓
#

Uses

  • Theorem 7

Lean declarations

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Jacobian

The Jacobian of \(C\) is the abelian variety

\[ \Jac (C) \; =\; \Pic ^0_{C/k}, \]

the connected component of the identity of the Picard scheme \(\Pic _{C/k}\) representing the relative Picard functor (Definition 6, Theorem 7). It is viewed as an object of \(\textrm{Over}(\Spec k)\).

All four properties are part of the conclusion of Theorem 7: once \(\Pic ^0_{C/k}\) is constructed as an abelian scheme of dimension \(g(C)\), smoothness, properness and geometric irreducibility hold by construction. The dimension formula in Theorem 10 additionally needs the Phase A identification of \(g(C) = \dim _k H^1(C, \mathcal O_C)\) with the dimension of the tangent space at the identity of \(\Pic ^0_{C/k}\) (Riemann–Roch / Serre duality).

5.2.1 Sanity check in low genus

  • \(g = 0\): Then \(\Pic ^0_{C/k} = \Spec k\), the trivial group scheme; \(\Jac (\mathbb P^1_k) = \Spec k\) is smooth of relative dimension \(0 = g\). The terminal object of \(\textrm{Over}(\Spec k)\) already carries GrpObj via instTensorUnit.

  • \(g = 1\): Then \(\Jac (C) \cong C\) once a \(k\)-rational point on \(C\) is fixed; \(C\) is itself an abelian variety.

  • \(g \ge 2\): \(\Jac (C)\) is a non-trivial \(g\)-dimensional abelian variety, never isomorphic to \(C\) as a scheme.

5.3 Mathlib gap

The Picard scheme is absent from Mathlib b80f227. Mathlib has only CommRing.Pic R (the Picard group of a commutative ring) and the abstract GrpObj machinery. Constructing Jacobian C therefore requires building, inside this project, the Picard scheme machinery of Chapter 4:

  • the line-bundle / Picard-group API on a single scheme — Chapter 3 (closed iter-003 under documented first-approximation);

  • the relative Picard functor (Definition 6) — Chapter 4 (scaffolded iter-004, definition assigned to iter-005 prover);

  • étale sheafification of group-valued functors on \(\mathrm{Sch}/k\) — iter-006+;

  • representability for the curve case (Theorem 7) — FGA-level, deferred indefinitely;

  • the connected-component-of-identity construction for group schemes locally of finite type — iter-006+.

These are mapped to Phase C of STRATEGY.md.

5.3.1 Status note

The four Jacobian.lean sorries (Jacobian, instGrpObj, instIsProper, instGeometricallyIrreducible, plus smoothOfRelativeDimension_genus which additionally depends on Phase A) all reduce to Theorem 7. They are not assigned to any prover until that theorem is honestly closed (which is gated on multiple Mathlib chapters: étale sheafification, Hilbert / Quot schemes, Riemann–Roch). Iter-005 will not attack them.
