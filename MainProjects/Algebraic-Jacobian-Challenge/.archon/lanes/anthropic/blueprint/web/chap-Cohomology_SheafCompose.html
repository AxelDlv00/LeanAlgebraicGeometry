1 Sheaf condition along the structure-sheaf forget composite

This chapter records the first step of Phase A in STRATEGY.md: equipping the abstract Mathlib sheaf-cohomology pipeline with the link it needs to compute \(H^1(C, \mathcal O_C)\) as an abelian group. It is the smallest unblocking unit of progress toward Definition 2 (the genus of a smooth proper curve).

The chapter introduces no protected declarations: every definition and lemma here is auxiliary, supporting the construction of \(H^1(C, \mathcal O_C)\) in subsequent Phase A iterations.

1.1 Statement

Mathlib has the abstract Grothendieck-topology sheaf-cohomology API CategoryTheory.Sheaf.H : Sheaf J AddCommGrpCat → ℕ → Type w’, but the structure sheaf \(\mathcal O_C\) of a scheme is delivered by Mathlib in the shape TopCat.Sheaf CommRingCat C (i.e. \(\mathrm{CommRing}\)-valued, not \(\mathrm{Ab}\)-valued). To plug \(\mathcal O_C\) into the cohomology API we must first transport the sheaf condition along the forgetful composite \(\mathrm{CommRing} \to \mathrm{Ring} \to \mathrm{Ab}\). The HasSheafCompose typeclass packages exactly this transport.

Lean declarations

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Cohomology.instHasSheafCompose_forget_CommRing_AddCommGrp

Let \(X\) be a topological space. Then the Grothendieck topology of opens of \(X\) has

\[ \mathtt{HasSheafCompose}\bigl( \mathrm{forget}_2\, \mathrm{CommRingCat}\, \mathrm{RingCat} \; \circ \; \mathrm{forget}_2\, \mathrm{RingCat}\, \mathrm{AddCommGrpCat} \bigr). \]

In Lean: instance (X : TopCat) : (TopologicalSpace.Opens.grothendieckTopology X).HasSheafCompose (CategoryTheory.forget₂ CommRingCat RingCat ⋙ CategoryTheory.forget₂ RingCat AddCommGrpCat)

1.2 Proof sketch

Proof ▶

Both \(\mathrm{forget}_2 \mathrm{CommRingCat}\, \mathrm{RingCat}\) and \(\mathrm{forget}_2 \mathrm{RingCat}\, \mathrm{AddCommGrpCat}\) are algebraic structure-forgetful functors and hence create (in particular preserve) all small limits. The sheaf condition for the topology of opens is expressed as preservation of certain limits of the form multiequalizer (Cover.index ...) (see Presheaf.IsSheaf.isSheaf_iff_isSheaf_of_type and the multispan-limit reformulation IsSheaf.isLimit_multifork). Composing two limit-preserving functors gives a limit-preserving functor, so the post-composition with the forget composite preserves the sheaf condition. The HasSheafCompose typeclass is exactly the resulting "sheaf-of-rings becomes sheaf-of-abelian-groups" lemma.

1.3 Use in the project

Theorem 1 is the gateway to:

  • the construction of \(\mathcal O_C\) as a sheaf of abelian groups on \(C\), by post-composing \(\mathcal O_C : \mathrm{Opens}(C)^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathrm{CommRingCat}\) with the forget composite — the resulting object lives in \(\mathrm{Sheaf}(\mathrm{Opens.gT}\, C, \mathrm{AddCommGrpCat})\) thanks to the typeclass above;

  • plugging that sheaf into CategoryTheory.Sheaf.H once HasSheafify (Opens.gT C) AddCommGrpCat and HasExt are available (Phase A steps 2–3);

  • endowing the resulting \(H^1\) with a \(k\)-vector-space structure via the \(k\)-algebra structure on \(\Gamma (C, \mathcal O_C)\) (Phase A step 4);

  • finite-dimensionality via Serre’s theorem on coherent cohomology of proper \(k\)-schemes (Phase A step 5);

  • Definition 2 (\(g(C) = \dim _k H^1(C, \mathcal O_C)\)) which then becomes formalisable.

The chain is: HasSheafCompose (this chapter) \(\to \) HasSheafify \(\to \) HasExt \(\to \) \(\mathrm{Module}\, k\)-on-\(H^1\) \(\to \) Serre finiteness \(\to \) genus.

1.4 Mathlib gap

Mathlib b80f227 has all the constituent pieces:

  • CategoryTheory.GrothendieckTopology.HasSheafCompose — the typeclass to populate.

  • CategoryTheory.HasForget₂ for \(\mathrm{CommRingCat} \to \mathrm{RingCat}\) (CommRingCat.hasForgetToRingCat) and \(\mathrm{RingCat} \to \mathrm{AddCommGrpCat}\) (RingCat.hasForgetToAddCommGrp).

  • Limit-preservation lemmas for algebraic forgetful functors and the sheaf-condition reformulations.

What is missing and supplied by this chapter: the assembled HasSheafCompose instance for the specific composite \(\mathrm{CommRingCat} \to \mathrm{AddCommGrpCat}\) on the Grothendieck topology of opens of an arbitrary topological space.

  2. Establish that the composite \(\mathrm{forget}_2\, \mathrm{CommRingCat}\, \mathrm{RingCat} \circ \mathrm{forget}_2\, \mathrm{RingCat}\, \mathrm{AddCommGrpCat}\) preserves the relevant limits, by appealing to existing Mathlib facts about algebraic forget functors (HasLimits preservation).

  3. Combine the two to produce the instance and close the sorry. The body should be at most \(\sim 20\) lines.

  4. Do not introduce a placeholder instance := sorry’ or any axiom: leave the sorry in place rather than weakening the definition.
