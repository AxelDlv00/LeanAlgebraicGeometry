### `regularLocal_inductive_step` (L973 helper, new private lemma)

Signature: given `R` regular local of dim `k + 1` plus the inductive hypothesis
at dim `k` (universally quantified over the ring), produce a regular sequence
of length `k + 1` in `maximalIdeal R`.

The helper body is a typed `sorry` packaging three Mathlib gaps:

1. **(Stacks 00NQ)** `IsRegularLocalRing R ⟹ IsDomain R`. The classical proof
   uses "no embedded prime in `(0)`" + zero-ideal-prime argument (~300 LOC of
   project work).
2. **(Stacks 00NU)** For `x ∈ 𝔪 \ 𝔪²`, `R ⧸ (x)` is again a regular local ring
   of dimension `k`. Needs Krull's principal ideal theorem applied to the
   quotient via the `spanFinrank` invariant; the maximal ideal of `R ⧸ (x)` is
   `𝔪 / (x)`, generated by the images of `x_2, …, x_{k+1}` from a minimal
   generating set of `𝔪` including `x` (~200 LOC).
3. **(Cons assembly bridge)** `QuotSMulTop x R ≅_{R/(x)} R ⧸ Ideal.span {x}` —
   the iso between the linear-algebra `M ⧸ (x • ⊤)` and the ring quotient
   `R ⧸ Ideal.span {x}` as `R/(x)`-modules. Pieces exist in Mathlib
   (`ideal_span_singleton_smul`, `quotEquivOfEq`) but the assembly into the
   right `R/(x)`-linear iso is multi-step. The cons step itself uses
   `RingTheory.Sequence.IsRegular.cons'` (Mathlib has this).
