## Part 1: Source Audit — What Nitsure §3–§5 and Kleiman §4 Actually Do

### Nitsure §1–§5 construction spine (read directly)

**§1 (pp. 1–5) — Quot functor definition.**
`Quot^{Φ,L}_{E/X/S}(T)` = equivalence classes ⟨F, q⟩ where:
- `F` is a coherent `O_{X_T}`-module, schematic support proper over T, flat over T
- `q : E_T ↠ F` surjective
- Hilbert polynomial of `F|_{X_t}` w.r.t. `L|_{X_t}` equals `Φ` at every `t ∈ T`

The **Hilbert polynomial** `Φ(m) = χ(F(m)) = Σ_i (-1)^i dim H^i(X_s, F|_{X_s} ⊗ L_s^{⊗m})` is
polynomial in m by Snapper's Lemma (Nitsure cites Kleiman [K]). This is a geometric Hilbert
polynomial over a field, not the ring-theoretic `Polynomial.hilbertPoly` in Mathlib.

**§2 (pp. 9–12) — Castelnuovo-Mumford m-regularity.**
`F` is m-regular if `H^i(ℙ^n, F(m-i)) = 0` for all `i ≥ 1`. Key Lemma 2.1: if F is m-regular
then (a) the multiplication map `H^0(O(1)) ⊗ H^0(F(r)) → H^0(F(r+1))` is surjective for r≥m,
(b) `H^i(F(r)) = 0` for i≥1 and r≥m−i, (c) `F(r)` is generated by global sections for r≥m.
The proof is induction on n via hyperplane restriction + long exact sequences.

The key theorem (Theorem 2.3): if `F` is a coherent quotient of `⊕^p O_{ℙ^n}(a)` then there
exists a polynomial `F_{p,n}(x_0,…,x_n)` — depending only on p, n, and the Hilbert polynomial
coefficients (a_0,…,a_n) — such that `F` is `F_{p,n}(a_0,…,a_n)`-regular. This gives a
**uniform regularity bound** for all quotients with given Hilbert polynomial — this is the
boundedness input to the Quot representability proof.

**§3 (pp. 13–17) — Semi-continuity and base-change.**
Lemma 3.1 (base-change without flatness): for `φ : T → S` noetherian, `F` coherent on `ℙ^n_S`,
there exists r_0 such that `φ* π_{S*} F(r) → π_{T*} F_T(r)` is an isomorphism for r≥r_0.
Lemma 3.2: if `π_* F(r)` is locally free for r≥N, then F is flat over S.
Theorem 3.3: **Grothendieck complex** — for `π : X → S` proper, S=Spec A affine, `F` flat:
∃ finite complex `0 → K^0 → K^1 → … → K^n → 0` of finitely generated projective A-modules
with `H^p(X, F ⊗_A M) ≅ H^p(K ⊗_A M)` functorially. This is the engine for everything below.
Theorem 3.7: **Semi-continuity** — for π proper, F coherent, F flat over S:
(1) `s ↦ dim_κ(s) H^i(X_s, F_s)` is upper semi-continuous;
(2) `s ↦ χ(F_s)` is locally constant;
(3) if `dim H^i(X_s, F_s)` is constant = d, then `R^i π_* F` is locally free of rank d.

**§4 (pp. 18–22) — Generic flatness and flattening stratification.**
Lemma 4.1 (Generic Flatness over a domain): for noetherian domain A, finite-type A-algebra B,
finite B-module M, ∃ f≠0 in A with M_f free over A_f.
Theorem 4.2: over noetherian integral S, coherent `F` on finite-type `X → S` is flat over a
non-empty open U ⊆ S.
**Theorem 4.3 (Flattening Stratification)**: for S noetherian, `F` coherent on `ℙ^n_S`, ∃ finite
locally-closed stratification {S_f}_f by locally-closed subschemes of S indexed by polynomials
f ∈ ℚ[λ], such that: (i) the Hilbert polynomial of `F` over S_f is f at every point; (ii) `i*F`
is flat over S' = ∐ S_f, and any φ: T → S with φ*F flat factors through i; (iii) closure condition.
The proof builds the strata as iterated flattening of `E_i = π_* F(N+i)` for i = 0,…,n.

**§5 (pp. 23–27) — Construction of Quot schemes.**
Main theorem (Theorem 5.2, Altman-Kleiman): for S noetherian, X = ℙ(V) (closed in ℙ(V)), E a
coherent quotient of π*(W)(ν), Φ ∈ ℚ[λ], `Quot^{Φ,L}_{E/X/S}` is representable by a scheme
embeddable as a closed subscheme of ℙ(F) for some vector bundle F on S.

**Four-step proof:**
1. **Reduction** (Lemma 5.4): reduce to X = ℙ(V), E = π*(W).
2. **Boundedness** (via m-regularity, §2): ∃ uniform m (depending only on rank(V), rank(W), Φ)
   such that for any field-valued point s and any coherent quotient q: E_s ↠ F on ℙ(V)_s with
   Hilbert polynomial Φ, the sheaves E_s, F, ker(q) are all m-regular. Consequence (*):
   π_{T*} G(r), π_{T*} E_T(r), π_{T*} F(r) locally free for r≥m; base-change isomorphisms hold;
   higher direct images vanish.
3. **Grassmannian embedding** (fix r≥m): the map
   `α : Quot^{Φ,L}_{E/X/S} → Grass(W ⊗_{O_S} Sym^r V, Φ(r))`
   sending ⟨F, q⟩ ↦ ⟨π_{T*}F(r), π_{T*}q(r)⟩ is injective (q is recoverable as cokernel).
4. **Flattening stratification to locally closed embedding**: taking T = Grass(W⊗Sym^r V, Φ(r))
   with universal quotient u: p_G*E → U, apply Theorem 4.3 to the coherent sheaf F = coker(h)
   on X_T; the Φ-stratum T' = T^Φ_0 of S = T is locally closed and represents `Quot^{Φ,L}_{E/X/S}`.
   Since Grass embeds as a closed subscheme of ℙ(⋀^{Φ(r)} (W⊗Sym^r V)), `Quot^{Φ,L}_{E/X/S}`
   embeds in ℙ(⋀^{Φ(r)} (…)).
5. **Valuative criterion for properness**: for R a DVR with fraction field K, the restriction
   `Quot^{Φ,L}_{E/X/S}(Spec R) → Quot^{Φ,L}_{E/X/S}(Spec K)` is bijective (flat + torsion-free
   over DVR argument). Hence Quot is proper, so the locally closed embedding is in fact closed.

### Kleiman §4 construction: what it actually invokes

**Theorem 4.8 (Main, pp. 27–31) proof spine:**
1. Write `P := Pic_{(X/S)(ét)}`. For each φ ∈ ℚ[n], define `P^φ ⊂ P` = étale subsheaf of
   invertible sheaves L on X_T with `χ(X_t, L_t^{-1}(n)) = φ(n)` for all t ∈ T. This uses
   flat base change for H^i (EGA III 7.7.5 / Theorem 3.7 Nitsure) to show `P^φ` is well-defined;
   it uses **R^i f_* and Serre's theorem (EGA III 2.2.2)** to show `P^φ` is open.
2. Further refine: for m ∈ ℤ, `P^φ_m ⊂ P^φ` = sub-presheaf with condition (4.8.2):
   `R^i f_*(L(n)) = 0` for all i≥1 and n≥m`. Show `P^φ_m` is well-defined and an isomorphism
   `ε: P^φ → P^φ_0` sending L ↦ L(m). The union of `P^φ_m` for m∈ℤ equals `P^φ`.
3. **Abel map**: `A_{X/S}: Div_{X/S} → P`, where `Div_{X/S}` is an open subscheme of **Hilb_{X/S}**
   by Kleiman §3 Theorem 3.7 (relative effective Cartier divisors = Hilb with Hilbert poly = 1).
   **Hilb_{X/S} = Quot^{1,L}_{O_X/X/S}** — the Hilbert scheme is a special case of the Quot scheme.
4. Form `Z = P^φ_0 ×_P Div_{X/S}`. Z is an open subscheme of `Div_{X/S}`, hence quasi-projective
   (inherits from Hilb which is quasi-projective from Quot theorem). The map `α: Z → P^φ_0` is
   surjective as a map of étale sheaves (proof: given T, a λ ∈ P^φ_0(T), form T' = P(Q) using
   the locally-free direct image Q from condition 4.8.2; then Q locally free means T'→T is smooth,
   and T'-points lift to Z-points).
5. **Lemma 4.9 (smooth-proper quotient)**: Let α: Z → P be étale sheaves, Z representable
   quasi-projective over S, R = Z×_P Z representable by S-scheme with first projection R → Z
   smooth and proper. Then P is representable by quasi-projective S-scheme. This is proved using
   faithfully flat descent (Lemma 6.7 = SGA 1 Exp. VIII Cor. 1.9).
6. Apply 4.9 to get: P^φ_0 is representable by a quasi-projective S-scheme. The Picard scheme
   Pic_{X/S} = ∐_φ P^φ is a disjoint union of quasi-projective S-schemes (by EGA 0 4.5.4).

**EXACT CONSTRUCTION KLEIMAN §4 INVOKES**: The **Hilbert scheme** `Hilb_{X/S}` (= `Quot^{1,L}_{O_X/X/S}`)
as the container for `Div_{X/S}`, plus **Lemma 4.9** (quotient of étale sheaves by smooth-proper
equivalence relation). This is NOT the direct Grassmannian-embedding of the full Quot. It uses
Quot for E=O_X only (Hilbert scheme), then gets representability via the quotient lemma.

**On the relative curve setting `C/k`:**
The curve C over k = Spec k satisfies the hypotheses of Theorem 4.8 directly: C → Spec k is
projective (hence Zariski-locally projective over S trivially), flat, with integral geometric fibers
(geometrically integral). The Quot functor `Quot^{Φ,L}_{O_C/C/k}` is the Hilbert scheme `Hilb^Φ_{C/k}`.
The parametrized family `C ×_k T → T` is the base change of C/k to T-schemes, which is
accommodated by Exercise (5) of Nitsure §1 (base change of Quot). So the Nitsure/Kleiman setup
**applies directly to the project's base**, without variant.

## Part 2: RR-Free Assessment

**CLAIM CONFIRMED: Representability of Pic_{C/k} via Kleiman §4 + Nitsure §5 is RR-free.**

Neither source uses Riemann-Roch at any step:
- Nitsure §5: uses CM-regularity (§2), semi-continuity (§3), flattening stratification (§4), and DVR-flatness for the valuative criterion.
- Kleiman §4: uses flat base change for H^i, Serre's finiteness (EGA III 2.2.2 — this is a finiteness theorem, not RR), the Hilbert scheme (from §3 of Kleiman + Nitsure §5), and the smooth-proper quotient lemma.

**Where "Riemann-Roch-free" must not be conflated with "cohomology-free":**
Kleiman §4 extensively uses higher cohomology `R^i f_*` for the decomposition into P^φ-strata.
This is not Riemann-Roch but it is nontrivial sheaf cohomology infrastructure. The project's
current Lean formalization (`canonicalBaseChangeMap`, `flatBaseChangeCohomology`) attempts to
cover the i=0 case only; the i≥1 cases needed for `P^φ` well-definedness and openness are
explicitly deferred.

## Part 4: Sub-Phase Decomposition

Each sub-phase lists: mathematical content, rough LOC, and gating prerequisites.

### A. R^i f_* and Cohomological Foundations
**Statement**: For `f : X → S` proper qcqs, define `R^i f_*` on `Scheme.Modules`; prove Serre
vanishing on projective space; prove Grothendieck complex existence (Theorem 3.3).
**LOC estimate**: 600–1000
**Prerequisites**: None beyond present Mathlib
**Status**: ABSENT. This is the deepest gap and gates B, D, F, I.
**Lean expressibility**: Expressible but requires building derived-category-style infrastructure
or Čech cohomology for coherent sheaves on noetherian schemes.

### B. Geometric Hilbert Polynomial and Snapper's Lemma
**Statement**: For `F` coherent, proper-support, with relatively ample `L`, the function
`m ↦ χ(F ⊗ L^⊗m)` is a polynomial in `m` (i.e., `Φ_F ∈ Polynomial ℚ` exists and equals
the Hilbert polynomial).
**LOC estimate**: 200–400
**Prerequisites**: Sub-phase A (R^i f_*)
**Status**: ABSENT. Mathlib's `Polynomial.hilbertPoly` is ring-theoretic (graded Hilbert series);
the geometric version is a different object.

### C. Castelnuovo-Mumford m-Regularity
**Statement**: Definition of m-regular coherent sheaf on `ℙ^n_k`; Lemma 2.1 (global generation +
cohomology vanishing for r≥m); Theorem 2.3 (uniform regularity bound for quotients with given
Hilbert polynomial depending only on polynomial coefficients and rank).
**LOC estimate**: 300–500
**Prerequisites**: Sub-phase A (Serre vanishing, H^i on projective spaces)
**Status**: ABSENT. No CM regularity in Mathlib in algebraic-geometry form.

### D. Semi-Continuity Theorem
**Statement**: Theorem 3.7 — for `π : X → S` proper, `F` coherent and flat: (1) `s ↦ h^i(F_s)`
upper semi-continuous; (2) `χ(F_s)` locally constant; (3) if `h^i` constant then `R^i π_* F`
locally free with base-change iso.
**LOC estimate**: 300–500
**Prerequisites**: Sub-phase A (Grothendieck complex, R^i f_*)
**Status**: ABSENT.

### E. Generic Flatness and Flattening Stratification
**Statement**: Lemma 4.1 (generic flatness over domain) + Theorem 4.3 (flattening stratification

**LOC estimate**: 500–800
**Prerequisites**: Sub-phases B (Hilbert polynomial), D (semi-continuity), A (R^i)
**Status**: ABSENT. `FlatteningStratification.lean` has typed-sorry placeholders with correct

### F. Grassmannian Scheme
**Statement**: For `S` noetherian, `V` locally free rank-r, `1 ≤ d ≤ r`: `Grass(V, d)` is

**LOC estimate**: 400–600
**Prerequisites**: Relative Proj `ℙ(E)` (absent), IsProjective (absent); DVR valuative criterion

## Part 5: Strategy-Modifying Findings

### Finding 1 (CONFIRMED CORRECT): RR-free claim is sound
Neither Nitsure §5 nor Kleiman §4 uses Riemann-Roch. The decomposition of Pic by Hilbert
polynomial is RR-free; the Hilbert polynomial here is defined as `χ(L_t^{-1}(n))` using the
Euler characteristic from flat base change (Theorem 3.7), not from RR. The strategy's claim is
accurate and is not a gap.

### Finding 2 (STRATEGY MODIFIER — CRITICAL): R^i f_* is a hidden prerequisite

**The STRATEGY's Mathlib-gap table lists:**
- CM m-regularity (absent ✓)
- Snapper's Lemma / Hilbert poly (absent ✓)
- R^i f_* higher direct images — **NOT LISTED** ⚠️

However, Sub-phases A through E all depend critically on `R^i f_*` for i≥1. In particular:
- The Grothendieck complex (Theorem 3.3) requires `R^i f_*` as its very definition
- The semi-continuity theorem (Theorem 3.7) is a theorem about `R^i f_*`
- Kleiman §4's decomposition of Pic into P^φ uses `H^i(X_t, L_t(n)) = 0` conditions (item 4.8.2)
  and the openness of P^φ uses EGA III 7.9.11 (Serre's finiteness theorem about higher cohomology)
- CM regularity is defined via `H^i(ℙ^n, F(m-i)) = 0`

The existing `canonicalBaseChangeMap` in `QuotScheme.lean` only addresses the i=0 flat base change.
The i≥1 form ("the full Stacks 02KH statement covers all R^i f_* F") is deferred as "post-iter-177."
This deferral is not a cosmetic lacuna — it blocks starting the core engine.

**Recommendation**: Add `R^i f_* for i≥1 on Scheme.Modules` as the **first entry** in the
Mathlib-gaps table. Evaluate whether this is best pursued as: (a) a Mathlib upstream PR
(most correct, ~6–12 months); (b) a project-side Čech cohomology build for the coherent case on
noetherian schemes (~800–1200 LOC, faster); or (c) a typed-sorry axiom for the sub-phases that
need it, treated as an explicit hypothesis.

### Finding 3 (STRATEGY MODIFIER — MODERATE): Relative Proj is absent and unlisted

Nitsure §5 (p. 23) begins: "A morphism X → S is called a projective morphism if there exists a
coherent sheaf E on S together with a closed embedding of X into `ℙ(E) = Proj Sym_{O_S} E`."
The STRATEGY currently uses `IsProper π` as a stand-in for `IsProjective`, with a comment
"once Mathlib gains an IsProjective morphism property." But the Quot proof requires not just the
predicate but the actual **relative Proj construction** `ℙ(E)`:
- To build the Grassmannian via the Plücker embedding `Grass(E, d) ↪ ℙ(⋀^d E)`, one needs
  `ℙ(⋀^d E)` as an actual scheme
- To state Theorem 5.2, one needs to know `Quot^{Φ,L}_{E/X/S}` embeds into `ℙ(F)` for some
  vector bundle F

Relative Proj is a significant construction (graded Spec, relative Proj of Sym algebra) and is
**ABSENT from Mathlib's scheme layer**. It should be added to the Mathlib-gap table.

**Estimated LOC for Relative Proj**: 400–700 LOC (graded-ring Spec + Proj for the Sym algebra +
universal property of relative Proj).

### Finding 4 (INFORMATIONAL): The Kleiman §4 route uses Hilbert scheme, not full Quot

The project's current framing ("Quot/Cartier engine") is slightly misleading. Kleiman §4's route
to representability uses:
1. The **Hilbert scheme** `Hilb_{X/S}` (Quot for E=O_X) as the container for Div_{X/S}
2. The **smooth-proper quotient lemma** (Lemma 4.9 = Theorem 6.8 of Nitsure) for the actual
   representability step

It does NOT directly embed Pic into a Quot or Grassmannian. The Quot scheme is a prerequisite only
insofar as Hilb = Quot (E=O_X) is its special case.

This is ALREADY correctly captured in `FGAPicRepresentability.lean`'s `smoothProperQuotient`
declaration, so no structural change is needed. However, the strategy document should clarify
that "Quot engine" means primarily "Hilbert scheme + smooth-proper quotient" for the §4 route.

### Finding 5 (INFORMATIONAL): LOC estimate confirmed, possibly underestimated

The STRATEGY estimates 2000–4000 LOC for the engine. The sub-phase table above gives 3400–5500
total. The gap relative to the STRATEGY estimate comes from: (a) R^i f_* (~600–1000 LOC not in
STRATEGY's estimate), (b) Relative Proj (~400–700 LOC not in STRATEGY's estimate). The "core"
engine phases (C, E, F, G, H, I) sum to ~2300–3600 LOC, consistent with the STRATEGY's estimate.
