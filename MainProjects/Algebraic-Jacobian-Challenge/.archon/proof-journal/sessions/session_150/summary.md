## Key findings / patterns discovered

### NEW Pattern — MvPolynomial pderiv coefficient formula at shifted monomial

`coeff (u - Finsupp.single i 1) (pderiv i f) = coeff u f * (u i : k)`
for `u i ≥ 1`. NOT in Mathlib. Proof goes by `MvPolynomial.as_sum`
+ `pderiv_monomial` + `Finset.sum_eq_single`; the singled-out summand
uses a finsupp-subtraction case-bash (helper
`_finsupp_sub_single_eq_of_one_le`). Reusable for any
joint-kernel-collapse or `pderiv`-via-monomials computation.

### NEW Pattern — `_mvPoly_mem_range_C_of_D_eq_zero` (joint-kernel collapse for `MvPolynomial σ k` in `CharZero`)

Closed via `mvPolynomialBasis_repr_apply` reducing to `∀ i, pderiv i f
= 0`, then the FREE-CASE joint-kernel collapse via the pderiv
coefficient formula. ~10 LOC of body + the helper machinery. The
FREE-CASE result is what HYBRID part (C) was after; the transfer
step to a general standard-smooth `B` is what remains.

### NEW Pattern — HYBRID-part-(B) consumer-compatibility blocker

When a planner directive recommends inflating a signature to add
`[CharZero k]` / `[PerfectField k]` for a CharZero-collapse closure,
verify the **consumer call sites** can supply the typeclass BEFORE
committing the inflation. The iter-150 instance: (S3.sep.\*) inflated
to `[PerfectField k]` closed locally in ~3 LOC each, but the
consumer `constants_integral_over_base_field` is in scope with only
`[Field k]` (not `[CharZero k]` and certainly not `[PerfectField k]`).
The clean fix is to propagate `[CharZero k]` through the consumer
first; the iter-150 lane could not commit this because it would
cascade upstream to `rigidity_over_kbar` and downstream consumers.

### NEW Anti-pattern — `Algebra.IsSeparable k F ⇒ Algebra.IsGeometricallyReduced k F` direction is NOT in Mathlib b80f227

Despite being mathematically true for finite separable field
extensions (`F ⊗ \bar k` splits as `∏ \bar k`), Mathlib b80f227 has
only the forward direction
(`Algebra.instIsReducedTensorProductOfIsAlgebraicOfIsGeometricallyReduced`).
Do NOT search for the reverse direction as a Mathlib instance — it
is genuinely a Mathlib-PR-grade gap. Add to "Known Blockers" so future
iters do not waste cycles on a Mathlib search that will return empty.
