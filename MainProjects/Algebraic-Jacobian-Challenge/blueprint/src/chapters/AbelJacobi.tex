\chapter{The Abel--Jacobi map}
\label{chap:AbelJacobi}
Let \(C\) be a smooth proper curve over \(k\) as in Chapter~\ref{chap:Genus}, and let \(\Jac(C)\) be its Jacobian (Definition~\ref{def:Jacobian}). The constructions of this chapter consume only the Albanese universal property of \(\Jac(C)\), as packaged by Definition~\ref{def:IsAlbanese} and provided uniformly in the marked point by Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}. The Lean formalisation closes every block in this chapter by a single projection from the Albanese structure \(\mathrm{IsAlbanese}\,C\,P\,\Jac(C)\); the corresponding classical line-bundle / Pic-scheme description is recorded as a remark for each block.
\section{Definition}
\begin{definition}\leanok
[Abel--Jacobi map]
  \label{def:ofCurve}
  \uses{def:Jacobian, def:IsAlbanese, def:IsAlbanese_ofCurve, thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}
  
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.Jacobian.ofCurve}
  Let \(P \colon \Spec k \to C\) be a \(k\)-rational point of \(C\), equivalently a morphism \(P \colon \mathbf 1 \to C\) in the category of \(k\)-schemes over \(\Spec k\). By Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness} the Jacobian \(\Jac(C)\) of Definition~\ref{def:Jacobian} carries, for each such marked point \(P\), an Albanese structure \(\mathrm{IsAlbanese}\,C\,P\,\Jac(C)\) in the sense of Definition~\ref{def:IsAlbanese}. The \emph{Abel--Jacobi map at \(P\)} is the universal pointed morphism \(\iota_P\) supplied by that Albanese structure:
  \[
    \alpha_P \;:=\; \iota_P \;\colon\; C \;\longrightarrow\; \Jac(C).
  \]
  Equivalently, \(\alpha_P\) is the projection of the universal-pointed-morphism field of the Albanese predicate \(\mathrm{IsAlbanese}\,C\,P\,\Jac(C)\), evaluated at the marked point \(P\).
\end{definition}
\begin{remark}
[Classical description (Pic-scheme route)]
  \label{rem:ofCurve_classical}
  Classically (cf.\ Hartshorne~III.4 and FGA Explained, Chapter~9), the Abel--Jacobi map at \(P\) admits the line-bundle description
  \[
    \alpha_P \colon C \;\longrightarrow\; \Pic^0_{C/k},\qquad Q \;\longmapsto\; [\mathcal O_C(Q - P)].
  \]
  Functorially, it is the morphism in the category of \(k\)-schemes induced by the line bundle \(\mathcal O_{C \times C}(\Delta - p_1^* P)\) on \(C \times C\) via the universal property of \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\) as the moduli space of degree-\(0\) line bundles on \(C\). The Lean formalisation does not follow this route: the Pic-scheme representability machinery (Route~A of the proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}, in particular sub-step~A.2) is not currently available in Mathlib. See Layer~I of Chapter~\ref{chap:Jacobian} for the formalisation route actually taken.
\end{remark}
\section{Pointed property}
\begin{lemma}\leanok
[\(\alpha_P\) sends \(P\) to \(0\)]
  \uses{def:ofCurve, lem:IsAlbanese_comp_ofCurve, thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}
  \label{lem:comp_ofCurve}
  
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.Jacobian.comp_ofCurve}
  \(P \;\circ\; \alpha_P \;=\; \eta[\Jac(C)]\), where \(\eta[\Jac(C)]\) is the identity element of the group scheme \(\Jac(C)\).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  The Lean closure is the field projection from the Albanese structure \(\mathrm{IsAlbanese}\,C\,P\,\Jac(C)\) supplied by Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}. By Definition~\ref{def:IsAlbanese}, this predicate carries among its data the assertion \(P \circ \iota_P = \eta_{\Jac(C)}\) that the universal pointed morphism sends the marked point to the identity of the target group scheme. Since \(\alpha_P := \iota_P\) by Definition~\ref{def:ofCurve}, the claim is precisely the pointed-property field of that Albanese structure.
\end{proof}
\begin{remark}
[Classical description]
  \label{rem:comp_ofCurve_classical}
  Under the classical Pic-scheme description of Remark~\ref{rem:ofCurve_classical}, the same equality reads at the level of \(k\)-points as
  \[
    \alpha_P(P) \;=\; [\mathcal O_C(P - P)] \;=\; [\mathcal O_C] \;=\; 0 \in \Pic^0_{C/k}(k),
  \]
  and globally as the statement that restricting the universal line bundle \(\mathcal O_{C \times C}(\Delta - p_1^* P)\) along the section \((P, \mathrm{id}) \colon C \to C \times C\) gives the trivial line bundle on \(C\), hence the zero point of \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\). This classical content is not used in the Lean formalisation.
\end{remark}
\section{Universal (Albanese) property}
\begin{theorem}\leanok
  \uses{def:ofCurve, def:IsAlbanese, def:Jacobian, lem:IsAlbanese_exists_unique_ofCurve_comp, thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}
[Albanese property of the Jacobian]
  \label{thm:exists_unique_ofCurve_comp}
  
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.Jacobian.exists_unique_ofCurve_comp}
  Let \(A\) be an abelian variety over \(k\) --- i.e.\ a smooth proper geometrically irreducible group scheme over \(k\). For every pointed morphism \(f \colon C \to A\) with \(f \circ P = \eta[A]\), there exists a unique group-scheme morphism \(g \colon \Jac(C) \to A\) such that \(f = \alpha_P \circ g\).
  \[
  \begin{array}{ccc}
    C & \stackrel{f}{\longrightarrow} & A \\
    {\scriptstyle\alpha_P} \downarrow & \nearrow {\scriptstyle\exists ! g} & \\
    \Jac(C) & &
  \end{array}
  \]
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  The argument has two stages: the Lean closure (a direct projection from an Albanese witness) and the classical description (a Pic-scheme / rigidity argument that the Lean side does not replay).
  \emph{Lean closure.} By Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}, the Jacobian \(\Jac(C)\) is, uniformly in the marked point \(P\), the underlying scheme of an Albanese witness for the pointed curve \((C, P)\): it carries an Albanese structure \(\mathrm{IsAlbanese}\,C\,P\,\Jac(C)\) in the sense of Definition~\ref{def:IsAlbanese}, whose universal pointed morphism is the Abel--Jacobi map \(\alpha_P\) of Definition~\ref{def:ofCurve} (with \(\alpha_P \circ P = \eta_{\Jac(C)}\) being Lemma~\ref{lem:comp_ofCurve}). The universal-property data carried by this Albanese structure is, by Definition~\ref{def:IsAlbanese}, exactly the existence-and-uniqueness assertion of the theorem: for every \(f \colon C \to A\) with \(f \circ P = \eta_A\), there is a unique \(g \colon \Jac(C) \to A\) with \(f = \alpha_P \circ g\). The Lean side closes the goal by projecting this universal-property field of the Albanese witness \(\mathrm{IsAlbanese}\,C\,P\,\Jac(C)\), applied to the data \((f, hf)\).
  \emph{Classical description.} The classical proof of the same statement proceeds in two parts. In positive genus (\(g(C) > 0\)), the existence and uniqueness of the factorisation come from the Pic-scheme representability theorem together with the Stein factorisation of the Abel--Jacobi morphism: see Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}, Route~A (sub-steps A.1--A.4) for the line-bundle / universal-property route via \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\), and Route~B (sub-steps B.1--B.3) for the symmetric-power / Stein-factorisation route. In genus \(0\), the same statement reduces to the rigidity theorem for \(C \to A\) --- the named declaration \cref{thm:rigidity_over_kbar} of \cref{chap:RigidityKbar}. This rigidity result is established \emph{over the algebraic closure \(\bar k\)}: its signature carries an \(\mathrm{IsAlgClosed}\,\bar k\) hypothesis, and it is built out of the shared cotangent-vanishing pile pieces (i)+(ii)+(iii) over the algebraically closed base. The resulting equality of morphisms is then descended back to the original base field \(k\) by faithfully-flat descent: because \(\Spec \bar k \to \Spec k\) is faithfully flat, two \(k\)-morphisms that agree after base change to \(\bar k\) already agree over \(k\) (\texttt{AlgebraicGeometry.Flat.epi\_of\_flat\_of\_surjective}). See Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}, sub-cases C.1--C.3, in particular the seven-step expansion C.2.a--C.2.g, where sub-step C.2.f (faithfully-flat descent of morphism equality from \(\bar k\) back to \(k\)) is the descent step carrying the algebraically-closed-base rigidity conclusion down to the original base field; a base-change-to-\(\bar k\) step (establishing rigidity) followed by this descent step is on the M2 critical path. The protected signature of \cref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness} does \emph{not} assume \(C(k) \neq \emptyset\), so the genus-\(0\) argument branches on whether a \(k\)-rational point exists. When \(C(k) \neq \emptyset\), the supplied \(P \in C(k)\) feeds the pointing hypothesis of \cref{thm:rigidity_over_kbar} directly --- no \(C \cong \mathbb P^1_k\) identification is required, sidestepping the Brauer--Severi obstruction (Brauer--Severi conics over \(\mathbb Q\) are the standard counterexample to an unconditional \(k\)-side identification). When \(C(k) = \emptyset\), the \(\mathtt{isAlbaneseFor}\) field of the genus-\(0\) witness (\cref{def:genusZeroWitness}) is vacuously true at the Lean type level: the universal quantifier \(\forall\, (P \colon \mathbf{1} \to C)\) ranges over the empty type. None of this classical content is replayed in the Lean formalisation: it is bundled into the deferred existence hypothesis \cref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}, whose proof is the project's single explicit foundational gap.
\end{proof}
\section{Implementation route via the Albanese framework}
The three results above are formalised through the Albanese framework of Chapter~\ref{chap:Jacobian}. In the absence of Mathlib's Pic-scheme machinery, the Jacobian is defined (Layer~I) as the underlying scheme of an Albanese witness uniform in the marked point (Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}); the universal-property data \((\iota_P,\, \text{factorisation})\) is then carried by the Albanese predicate (Definition~\ref{def:IsAlbanese}) and projects directly into the three results of this chapter (Layer~II): the Abel--Jacobi map \(\alpha_P := \iota_P\) of Definition~\ref{def:ofCurve}, its pointed property of Lemma~\ref{lem:comp_ofCurve}, and the universal factorisation of Theorem~\ref{thm:exists_unique_ofCurve_comp}. Existence of a uniform-over-\(P\) Albanese object is the single remaining mathematical hypothesis pending the formalisation of either Pic-scheme representability (Route~A), symmetric-power quotients plus Stein factorisation (Route~B), or rigidity for \(C \to A\) proved over the algebraic closure \(\bar k\) and descended to \(k\) (genus-\(0\) sub-case, via \cref{thm:rigidity_over_kbar}, whose signature carries an \(\mathrm{IsAlgClosed}\,\bar k\) hypothesis; the equality of morphisms obtained over \(\bar k\) is brought back to \(k\) by faithfully-flat descent of morphism equality, which is precisely sub-step C.2.f of \S~C.2).
The classical proof via line bundles --- using the universal property of \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\), dual abelian varieties, and (for the genus-\(0\) sub-case) rigidity for \(C \to A\) proved over the algebraic closure \(\bar k\) via \cref{thm:rigidity_over_kbar} and descended to \(k\) by faithfully-flat descent of morphism equality (\texttt{AlgebraicGeometry.Flat.epi\_of\_flat\_of\_surjective}) --- gives the same conclusions but requires more Mathlib infrastructure than is presently available, as catalogued in the proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness} and the milestone roadmap of \texttt{STRATEGY.md}. The Albanese-framework route used here is mathematically equivalent and bundles all of the missing infrastructure into that single deferred existence hypothesis.
