\chapter{The Jacobian as an abelian variety}
\label{chap:Jacobian}
Let \(C\) be a smooth proper curve over \(k\) in the sense of Chapter~\ref{chap:Genus}, with genus \(g = g(C)\).
The Jacobian is built as the Albanese object of \(C\) in the category of smooth proper group schemes over \(k\), and the construction stratifies by genus. For positive genus the witness \emph{object} is the abelian variety \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\) produced by \textbf{Route~A (the Picard scheme via FGA representability)}: this is the project's committed critical path, and it is \emph{mandatory} for the positive-genus object regardless of any other route choice --- the object must be a genuine \(g\)-dimensional abelian variety even when \(C(k) = \emptyset\) (see \cref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness} Route~A and \cref{def:positiveGenusWitness}). The FGA representability for \(\Pic_{C/k}\) and its Hilbert/Quot prerequisites are the single largest Mathlib build-out the project gates on. For genus \(0\) the witness object is instead the trivial scheme \(\Spec k\), \textbf{decoupled} from Route~A's representability engine; the only remaining content in that arm is a rigidity \emph{statement}, the characteristic-free keystone \cref{prop:rigidity_genus0_curve_to_AV} developed in \cref{chap:AbelianVarietyRigidity} via the Milne \S I.3 rigidity chain (Rigidity Lemma \(+\) additivity Corollary~1.5 \(+\) Corollary~1.2 \(+\) rational-map extension), \emph{cube-free} and \emph{not} the differential / Serre-duality argument. The over-\(\bar k\) declaration \cref{thm:rigidity_over_kbar} of \cref{chap:RigidityKbar}, which still carries \([\mathtt{CharZero}]\), is the retained fallback-(a) artifact and is \emph{not} the declaration the genus-\(0\) witness consumes.
\section{The Albanese construction}
For a smooth proper curve \(C\) over \(k\), the Albanese variety is the universal abelian variety receiving a morphism from \(C\). Over an algebraically closed field this is classically identified with the connected component of the identity of the Picard scheme, but we construct it directly from symmetric powers.
\subsection{Symmetric powers and the Abel--Jacobi map}
Let \(C^{(g)} = \Sym^g(C)\) be the \(g\)\textsuperscript{th} symmetric power of \(C\) (the quotient of \(C^g\) by the action of the symmetric group \(S_g\)). It is a smooth proper scheme over \(k\) of dimension \(g\). Fix a \(k\)-rational point \(P_0 \in C(k)\). The map
\[
  \sigma \colon C^{(g)} \longrightarrow \Pic^g_{C/k}, \qquad
  \sum_{i=1}^g [P_i] \longmapsto \mathcal O_C\bigl(\textstyle\sum_{i=1}^g P_i - g P_0\bigr)
\]
is surjective and birational onto its image; its fibres are projective spaces (Brill--Noether--Riemann--Roch). Choosing \(P_0\) gives a translation isomorphism \(\Pic^g_{C/k} \xrightarrow{\;\sim\;} \Pic^0_{C/k}\), and the Stein factorisation of \(\sigma\) yields the Jacobian as the connected component of the identity.
In the Lean formalisation we do not have general symmetric powers of schemes (or quotients by finite group actions) in Mathlib. We therefore take a more direct approach: we define the Jacobian as the Albanese object of \(C\) inside the category of smooth proper group schemes over \(k\), using the universal property and the known dimension formula.
\subsection{The Albanese universal property}
\begin{definition}\leanok
[Albanese universal property of a pointed curve]
  \label{def:IsAlbanese}
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.IsAlbanese}
  % SOURCE: [Kleiman, The Picard scheme], Remark rmk:Alb (the Albanese map), FGA Explained ch.~5; arXiv:math/0504020, p.~50  (read from references/kleiman-picard-src/kleiman-picard.tex)
  % SOURCE QUOTE: "Reversing the preceding argument, we see that every
  % such \(b\) arises from a unique map \(a\:B^*\to \IPic_{X/k}\) such that
  % \(a(0)=0\).  Since \(B^*\) is integral, \(a\) factors through \(P\).  Thus the
  % maps \(a\:B^*\to P\) and \(b\:X\to B\) are in bijective correspondence.
  % Plainly, this correspondence is compatible with maps \(b'\:B\to B'\) such
  % that \(b'(0)=0\).  In particular, \(1_P\) corresponds to a natural map
  % \(u\:X\to A\) such that \(u(x)=0\), and every map \(b\:X\to B\) factors
  % uniquely through \(u\)."
  \textit{Source: Kleiman, The Picard Scheme, Remark (the Albanese map, rmk:Alb).}
  Let \((C, P)\) be a smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve over \(k\) with a marked \(k\)-rational point \(P \in C(k)\). A smooth proper geometrically irreducible group scheme \(J\) over \(k\) is an \emph{Albanese object} for \((C, P)\) if there exists a morphism \(\iota \colon C \to J\) sending \(P\) to the identity element \(\eta \in J\), and such that every morphism \(f \colon C \to A\) from \(C\) to a smooth proper geometrically irreducible group scheme \(A\) with \(f(P) = \eta_A\) factors uniquely as \(f = \iota \circ g\) for some morphism of group schemes \(g \colon J \to A\).
\end{definition}
\begin{remark}
  \label{rem:IsAlbanese_typeclasses}
  The Lean encoding of Definition~\ref{def:IsAlbanese} takes the four abelian-variety conditions on \(J\) --- group-object structure, properness, smoothness, geometric irreducibility --- as \emph{typeclass parameters} of the declaration \texttt{AlgebraicGeometry.IsAlbanese}, not as conjuncts inside the body. The body is the existential statement on \(\iota\) and its universal-factorisation property; the four hypotheses on \(J\) sit on the binder of the definition. A downstream user constructs an \texttt{IsAlbanese} term against these instances and threads them at every use site (cf.\ the \texttt{letI} chain in \texttt{AbelJacobi.ofCurve} which installs all four instances on \(\Jac(C)\) before applying any Albanese consequence). The same convention is used for the target abelian variety \(A\) in the universal-property clause.
\end{remark}
\subsection{Extracting the universal morphism}
An \texttt{IsAlbanese} term \(h\) packages, behind a single existential, the universal pointed morphism \(\iota \colon C \to J\) together with its two characterising properties: the pointed condition \(P \circ \iota = \eta_J\) and the universal-factorisation property of \(\iota\). The data and the two properties are extracted from the existential body by the following trio of helpers, each implemented in Lean via \texttt{Classical.choose} / \texttt{Classical.choose\_spec} on the existential content of \texttt{IsAlbanese}.
\begin{definition}
\leanok
[The Albanese universal morphism]
  \label{def:IsAlbanese_ofCurve}
  \uses{def:IsAlbanese}

  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.IsAlbanese.ofCurve}
  Given a pointed curve \((C, P)\) and an Albanese term \(h \colon \mathrm{IsAlbanese}\,C\,P\,J\), the \emph{universal morphism} of \(h\) is the morphism
  \[
    \iota := h.\mathtt{ofCurve} \colon C \to J,
  \]
  obtained by \texttt{Classical.choose} on the existential body of \(h\).
\end{definition}
\begin{lemma}
\leanok
[Pointed condition for the universal morphism]
  \label{lem:IsAlbanese_comp_ofCurve}
  \uses{def:IsAlbanese, def:IsAlbanese_ofCurve}

  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.IsAlbanese.comp_ofCurve}
  The universal morphism \(h.\mathtt{ofCurve}\) sends the marked point \(P\) to the identity of \(J\):
  \[
    P \circ h.\mathtt{ofCurve} \;=\; \eta_J.
  \]
\end{lemma}
\begin{lemma}
\leanok
[Universal factorisation through the Albanese object]
  \label{lem:IsAlbanese_exists_unique_ofCurve_comp}
  \uses{def:IsAlbanese, def:IsAlbanese_ofCurve}

  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.IsAlbanese.exists_unique_ofCurve_comp}
  For every morphism \(f \colon C \to A\) from \(C\) to a smooth proper geometrically irreducible group scheme \(A\) over \(k\) with \(P \circ f = \eta_A\), there exists a unique morphism \(g \colon J \to A\) such that \(f = h.\mathtt{ofCurve} \circ g\).
\end{lemma}
This extraction API is precisely what the protected interface \(\mathtt{Jacobian.ofCurve}\), \(\mathtt{Jacobian.comp\_ofCurve}\), \(\mathtt{Jacobian.exists\_unique\_ofCurve\_comp}\) of Chapter~\ref{chap:AbelJacobi} consumes: each protected wrapper takes a marked point \(P \in C(k)\) as input, projects the per-point Albanese data from the bundled witness \texttt{JacobianWitness} (Definition~\ref{def:JacobianWitness}) at that \(P\), and applies the corresponding helper above.
\begin{theorem}\leanok
[Uniqueness of the Albanese object]
  \label{thm:IsAlbanese_unique}
  \uses{def:IsAlbanese, def:IsAlbanese_ofCurve}
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.IsAlbanese.unique}
  Let \(J_1, J_2\) be two Albanese objects for the same pointed curve \((C, P)\), with universal pointed morphisms \(\iota_1 := h_1.\mathtt{ofCurve} \colon C \to J_1\) and \(\iota_2 := h_2.\mathtt{ofCurve} \colon C \to J_2\) (Definition~\ref{def:IsAlbanese_ofCurve}). Then there is a unique morphism \(e \colon J_1 \to J_2\) satisfying \(\iota_2 = \iota_1 \circ e\). The proof is the standard universal-property argument: each of the two universal morphisms factors through the other, the two composites equal the unique self-factorisation \(\mathrm{id}\), and the produced morphism \(e\) is therefore an isomorphism; only the existence and uniqueness of \(e\) as a morphism are exposed in the conclusion (see Remark~\ref{rem:IsAlbanese_unique_iso}).
\end{theorem}
\begin{remark}
  \label{rem:IsAlbanese_unique_iso}
  The Lean proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:IsAlbanese_unique} internally produces an inverse morphism \(h \colon J_2 \to J_1\) and verifies \(g \circ h = \mathrm{id}_{J_1}\) and \(h \circ g = \mathrm{id}_{J_2}\) (the invertibility witnesses are computed as intermediate lemmas of the tactic block) before returning the triple \((g,\ \iota_2 = \iota_1 \circ g,\ \text{uniqueness})\). The invertibility witnesses are computed but not retained in the return type
  \[
    \exists! (e : J_1 \to J_2),\ \iota_2 = \iota_1 \circ e.
  \]
  The natural strengthening would be to return
  \[
    \exists! (e : J_1 \cong J_2),\ \iota_2 = \iota_1 \circ e.\mathtt{hom},
  \]
  packaging the produced morphism together with its inverse. Tightening the conclusion in this way is a Lean-side refactor of the signature of \texttt{IsAlbanese.unique} that the project may carry out in a future iteration. The downstream consumers of \texttt{IsAlbanese.unique} (the protected \texttt{AbelJacobi.Jacobian.*} interface) use only the morphism-and-uniqueness content of the conclusion, so the current weaker conclusion is sufficient for the present API.
\end{remark}
\subsection{The Jacobian definition}
\begin{definition}\leanok
[Jacobian]
  \uses{def:genus, def:IsAlbanese, def:JacobianWitness, thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}
  \label{def:Jacobian}
  
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.Jacobian}
  The \emph{Jacobian} of \(C\) is the abelian variety \(\Jac(C)\) over \(k\) obtained as the Albanese object of \(C\): a smooth proper geometrically irreducible group scheme over \(k\) of dimension \(g(C)\) equipped with a universal morphism \(C \to \Jac(C)\) (for each choice of marked point). It is viewed as an object of the category of \(k\)-schemes over \(\Spec k\). Formally, \(\Jac(C)\) is defined as the underlying scheme of a (uniform-over-\(P\)) Albanese witness for \(C\) provided by Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}.
  When \(g(C) = 0\) the Jacobian collapses to \(\Spec k\) itself, which is the terminal object in the category of \(k\)-schemes. When \(g(C) > 0\) the underlying scheme is the abelian variety produced by Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}; the genus-one case recovers \(C\) itself (after choosing a \(k\)-rational point), and the higher genus case yields the non-trivial \(g\)-dimensional abelian variety. Both cases are handled uniformly: \(\Jac(C)\) is the underlying scheme of the Albanese witness, with no special-case branching at the level of the definition. The genus-\(0\) specialisation is implicit in the fact that any group scheme over \(k\) of relative dimension \(0\) is isomorphic to \(\Spec k\).
\end{definition}
\section{Group scheme structure and abelian-variety properties}
The four protected instances on \(\Jac(C)\) are each obtained, at the level of the Lean formalisation, by projecting a field of the underlying Albanese witness produced by Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}. The mathematical content of each property is the corresponding statement about the Albanese variety of a smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve.
\begin{theorem}\leanok
[Group object structure]
  \label{thm:Jacobian_grpObj}
  \uses{def:Jacobian, thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.Jacobian.instGrpObj}
  \(\Jac(C)\) carries a canonical group-object structure in the category of \(k\)-schemes over \(\Spec k\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  By Definition~\ref{def:Jacobian}, \(\Jac(C) = (\mathrm{jacobianWitness}\, C).J\) as a \(k\)-scheme, where \(\mathrm{jacobianWitness}\, C\) is the Albanese witness extracted from Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness} via \texttt{Classical.choice}. The witness carries the group-object structure as its field \(\mathtt{grpObj} : \mathrm{GrpObj}\, J\), so \(\Jac(C)\) inherits it by projection.
  Mathematically, any Albanese variety carries a unique group-object structure compatible with its universal pointed morphism. Indeed, if \(\iota \colon C \to J\) is the universal morphism sending the marked point \(P\) to the identity, then the diagonal \(C \times C \to J \times J\), post-composed with the universal morphism out of \(C \times C\) (factored through \(J\) by Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}), produces the multiplication; the unique morphism to a one-point pointed scheme produces the inverse; the marked point produces the identity. Uniqueness of the resulting group-object structure is again the universal-property argument of Theorem~\ref{thm:IsAlbanese_unique}.
\end{proof}

  \(\Jac(C) \to \Spec k\) is smooth of relative dimension \(g(C)\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  The Lean formalisation projects the field \(\mathtt{smoothGenus} : \mathrm{SmoothOfRelativeDimension}\,(\genus\, C)\,J.\mathtt{hom}\) of the witness \(\mathrm{jacobianWitness}\, C\); the relative dimension \(g(C)\) is recorded directly in the witness so that no separate dimension calculation is needed on the Lean side.
  Mathematically, the dimension of an Albanese variety of \(C\) equals the genus \(g = g(C) = \dim_k H^1(C, \mathcal O_C)\) (Phase~A, Chapter~\ref{chap:Cohomology_StructureSheafAb}). The tangent space to \(\Jac(C)\) at the identity is canonically identified with \(H^1(C, \mathcal O_C)\) by deformation theory of the Picard functor: an infinitesimal deformation of \(\mathcal O_C\) is classified by an element of \(H^1(C, \mathcal O_C)\), and this identifies the tangent space at the identity of \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\) with \(H^1(C, \mathcal O_C)\). Smoothness of \(\Jac(C)\) over \(k\) follows from the smoothness of any group scheme over a field whose tangent space at the identity is of constant rank equal to the relative dimension --- a consequence of homogeneity of the group action, since translation by any \(k\)-rational point is an automorphism and so the local structure of \(\Jac(C)\) at every \(k\)-rational point is the same as at the identity.
\end{proof}

  The Lean formalisation projects the field \(\mathtt{proper} : \mathrm{IsProper}\, J.\mathtt{hom}\) of the witness.
  Mathematically, properness of the Albanese variety is part of its construction. In the symmetric-power route, properness of \(\Pic^g_{C/k}\) (and hence \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\) after translation) follows from properness of \(\Sym^g(C)\) together with the universal closedness of the surjection \(\sigma\). In either case, properness descends to \(\Jac(C)\).
\end{proof}

\subsection{Sanity check in low genus}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \(g = 0\): Then \(\Jac(C) = \Spec k\), the trivial group scheme; \(\Jac(\mathbb P^1_k) = \Spec k\) is smooth of relative dimension \(0 = g\).
  \item \(g = 1\): Then \(\Jac(C) \cong C\) once a \(k\)-rational point on \(C\) is fixed; \(C\) is itself an elliptic curve (abelian variety of dimension \(1\)).
  \item \(g \ge 2\): \(\Jac(C)\) is a non-trivial \(g\)-dimensional abelian variety, never isomorphic to \(C\) as a scheme.
\end{itemize}
\section{Existence of an Albanese variety}
The classical construction of the Jacobian goes through the Picard scheme machinery: \(\Jac(C)\) is the connected component of the identity of \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\). An equivalent route, available whenever a \(k\)-rational point \(P \in C(k)\) has been fixed, is to take the Stein factorisation of the Abel--Jacobi morphism \(\Sym^g(C) \to \Pic^g_{C/k}\) and translate by \(-gP\).
\subsection{The Albanese witness bundle}
Before stating the existence theorem, we package its conclusion into a single structure --- the \emph{Albanese witness} of \(C\). This bundling reverses the quantifier order of the classical statement (see Remark~\ref{rem:JacobianWitness_quantifier_order}) and is the data structure consumed by the \texttt{Jacobian} family of declarations in this file and by the \texttt{AbelJacobi} interface of Chapter~\ref{chap:AbelJacobi}.
\begin{definition}
\leanok
  \uses{def:IsAlbanese, def:genus}
[Albanese witness for a curve]
  \label{def:JacobianWitness}
  
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.JacobianWitness}
  An \emph{Albanese witness} for the smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve \(C\) over \(k\) is a bundle of the following seven fields:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \(J\): an object of the category of \(k\)-schemes over \(\Spec k\) --- the candidate Albanese object.
    \item \(\mathtt{grpObj}\): a group-object structure on \(J\) in the category of \(k\)-schemes over \(\Spec k\).
    \item \(\mathtt{proper}\): a proof that the structure morphism \(J \to \Spec k\) is proper.
    \item \(\mathtt{smooth}\): a proof that the structure morphism \(J \to \Spec k\) is smooth.
    \item \(\mathtt{geomIrred}\): a proof that the structure morphism \(J \to \Spec k\) is geometrically irreducible.
    \item \(\mathtt{smoothGenus}\): a proof that the structure morphism \(J \to \Spec k\) is smooth of relative dimension \(g(C)\). This refines \(\mathtt{smooth}\) at the specific relative dimension \(\genus\, C\) (see Remark~\ref{rem:JacobianWitness_smooth_redundancy}).
    \item \(\mathtt{isAlbaneseFor}\): a proof that, for every \(k\)-rational marked point \(P \in C(k)\), the scheme \(J\) together with its group-object, proper, smooth, and geometrically-irreducible structures satisfies the Albanese universal property for the pointed curve \((C, P)\) in the sense of Definition~\ref{def:IsAlbanese}.
  \end{itemize}
  Formally, an Albanese witness is a Lean-level record (a structure) whose name is \texttt{JacobianWitness}.
\end{definition}
\begin{remark}
  \label{rem:JacobianWitness_quantifier_order}
  The classical phrasing of the existence statement of an Albanese variety is
  \[
    \forall\, P \in C(k),\ \exists\, J,\ J \text{ is an Albanese object for } (C, P).
  \]
  The witness bundle reverses these quantifiers: a single \(J\) is chosen once (intrinsic to \(C\)), and the marked-point-dependent data is recorded as the family-valued field
  \[
    \mathtt{isAlbaneseFor} \colon \forall\, P \in C(k),\ \mathrm{IsAlbanese}\,C\,P\,J.
  \]
  Mathematically this reversal is justified because the underlying scheme of any Albanese object is intrinsic to \(C\): a different choice of \(P \in C(k)\) alters the universal morphism \(\iota_P \colon C \to J\) by a translation of \(J\), which is an automorphism of \(J\), and so leaves \(J\) unchanged as a scheme. The reversed bundling is what makes the per-\(P\) projection in Chapter~\ref{chap:AbelJacobi} clean: \(\mathtt{Jacobian.ofCurve}\), \(\mathtt{Jacobian.comp\_ofCurve}\), and \(\mathtt{Jacobian.exists\_unique\_ofCurve\_comp}\) each take a marked point \(P\) as input and project the corresponding component of \(\mathtt{isAlbaneseFor}\,P\) via the extraction trio (Definition~\ref{def:IsAlbanese_ofCurve}, Lemmas~\ref{lem:IsAlbanese_comp_ofCurve}, \ref{lem:IsAlbanese_exists_unique_ofCurve_comp}).
\end{remark}
\begin{remark}
  \label{rem:JacobianWitness_smooth_redundancy}
  The fields \(\mathtt{smooth}\) and \(\mathtt{smoothGenus}\) of an Albanese witness are mathematically redundant: the relative-dimension version implies the unqualified version via Mathlib's \texttt{AlgebraicGeometry.SmoothOfRelativeDimension.smooth}. Keeping both fields is a Lean-level convenience: \(\mathtt{smooth}\) is the form required by the typeclass binder of \texttt{IsAlbanese} (and by the protected \(\mathtt{Jacobian.instGrpObj}\) projection), whereas \(\mathtt{smoothGenus}\) is the form required by the protected \(\mathtt{Jacobian.smoothOfRelativeDimension\_genus}\) projection. The redundancy carries no mathematical cost (the two fields agree) and no soundness cost (deriving one from the other would only re-run the same Mathlib instance synthesis at the projection sites).
  \uses{def:IsAlbanese, def:genus, def:JacobianWitness}
\end{remark}

  Let \(C\) be a smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve over a field \(k\). There exists a smooth proper geometrically irreducible group scheme \(J\) over \(k\) of relative dimension \(g(C)\) such that, for every \(k\)-rational point \(P \in C(k)\), the scheme \(J\) is an Albanese object for the pointed curve \((C, P)\) in the sense of Definition~\ref{def:IsAlbanese}. Equivalently, the type of Albanese witnesses for \(C\) (Definition~\ref{def:JacobianWitness}) is non-empty.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  The proof is split into three classical constructions plus the genus-\(0\) sub-case absorbed into the same witness (the latter packaged as the explicit construction \cref{def:genusZeroWitness}, which routes through the characteristic-free keystone \cref{prop:rigidity_genus0_curve_to_AV} of \cref{chap:AbelianVarietyRigidity} --- rigidity proved over the algebraic closure \(\bar k = \mathrm{AlgebraicClosure}\,k\) --- and then \emph{descends} the resulting morphism equality from \(\bar k\) to the base field \(k\) along the faithfully-flat surjection \(\Spec \bar k \to \Spec k\)). Each route produces the same intrinsic scheme \(J\); only the universal morphism \(\iota_P \colon C \to J\) varies with the marked point \(P\). We outline each route and name the Mathlib infrastructure each one requires.
  \paragraph{(0) Reduction to a \(P\)-independent witness.}
  The witness \(J\) depends only on \(C\), not on the marked point \(P\). Indeed, both classical constructions below produce \(J\) as an intrinsic object of \(C\) (the identity component of \(\Pic_{C/k}\), or the Stein-factorisation target of \(\Sym^g(C) \to \Pic^g_{C/k}\) translated by a fixed \(P_0\)); a different choice of \(P\) in \(C(k)\) alters \(\iota_P\) by a translation of \(J\), which is an automorphism, and so does not change \(J\) as a scheme. The Lean encoding reflects this by bundling \(J\) as a single field and the universal-property data as \(\forall P,\, \mathrm{IsAlbanese}\, C\, P\, J\). We now turn to producing \(J\).
  \paragraph{Route A --- Picard scheme.}
  This route follows Hartshorne~III.4 and FGA Explained, Chapter~9.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item[\textbf{(A.1)}] \emph{The relative Picard functor.} For a \(k\)-scheme \(T\), write \(C_T := C \times_k T\) and let \(\pi \colon C_T \to T\) be the projection. The relative Picard functor is
    \[
      \Pic^{\sharp}_{C/k} \colon (\Sch/k)^{\mathrm{op}} \to \Sets, \qquad
      T \longmapsto \Pic(C_T) / \pi^*\Pic(T).
    \]
    A \(T\)-point is a line bundle on \(C_T\) modulo a line bundle pulled back from \(T\). The set-valued formulation suffices for representability; the canonical abelian-group structure on \(\Pic(C_T)\) descends to the quotient by \(\pi^*\Pic(T)\) and gives \(\Pic^{\sharp}_{C/k}\) a natural group-functor refinement.
    \item[\textbf{(A.2)}] \emph{Representability.} For \(C\) smooth proper geometrically connected over \(k\) with a \(k\)-rational point, \(\Pic^{\sharp}_{C/k}\) (after \'etale sheafification, if \(k\) is not algebraically closed) is representable by a \(k\)-group scheme \(\Pic_{C/k}\), locally of finite type. This is FGA's main representability theorem (Grothendieck, FGA~232); the smooth-proper-geometrically-connected hypothesis ensures that the cohomology-and-base-change machinery applies and that \(H^0(C, \mathcal O_C) = k\), which is needed for the quotient by \(\pi^*\Pic(T)\) to be \'etale-locally injective.
    \item[\textbf{(A.3)}] \emph{The identity component.} Let \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\) be the identity component of \(\Pic_{C/k}\). As the identity component of a \(k\)-group scheme locally of finite type, \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\) is geometrically irreducible; for \(C\) smooth proper of genus \(g\), it is moreover a smooth proper \(k\)-group scheme of dimension \(g\). Smoothness comes from the deformation-theoretic identification of its tangent space at the identity with \(H^1(C, \mathcal O_C)\) and homogeneity of the group action; properness from the fact that the natural map \(\Pic^0_{C/k} \to \Pic_{C/k}\) is an open and closed immersion onto a proper component (a consequence of the cohomological criterion: \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\) is the kernel of the degree map \(\Pic_{C/k} \to \underline{\mathbb Z}_k\), which is locally constant). Take \(J := \Pic^0_{C/k}\).
    \item[\textbf{(A.4)}] \emph{The Abel--Jacobi universal morphism.} For each \(P \in C(k)\), the Abel--Jacobi morphism
    \[
      \iota_P \colon C \to \Pic^0_{C/k}, \qquad
      Q \longmapsto [\mathcal O_C(Q - P)]
    \]
    sends \(P\) to the identity of \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\). It is universal among morphisms \(f \colon C \to A\) to smooth proper geometrically irreducible \(k\)-group schemes \(A\) with \(f(P) = \eta_A\): this is the classical Albanese property of \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\), proved by reducing to the case \(k = \overline k\) (Galois descent), where it is a standard consequence of the autoduality of the Jacobian and the fact that line bundles on \(C \times A\) trivialised along \(\{P\} \times A\) correspond bijectively to morphisms \(C \to A^{\vee} = A\) sending \(P\) to \(\eta_A\).
  \end{itemize}

  \begin{itemize}
    \item[\textbf{(B.1)}] \emph{The \(g\)-fold symmetric power.} Form the product \(C^g\) and let \(S_g\) act by permutation of the factors. The categorical quotient \(C^{(g)} := C^g / S_g\) exists as a \(k\)-scheme, smooth and proper over \(k\) of dimension \(g\). Smoothness follows because the action of \(S_g\) on the smooth \(k\)-scheme \(C^g\) is free outside the diagonal locus and the local-ring computation at the diagonal points produces a smooth ring (this is the symmetric-product computation of Fogarty / Mumford). Properness descends from properness of \(C^g\) via the geometric-quotient property.
    \item[\textbf{(B.2)}] \emph{The Abel--Jacobi morphism on symmetric powers.} Fix a \(k\)-rational point \(P_0 \in C(k)\). The map
    \[
      \sigma \colon C^{(g)} \longrightarrow \Pic^g_{C/k}, \qquad
      \sum_{i=1}^g [Q_i] \longmapsto \mathcal O_C\bigl(\textstyle\sum_{i=1}^g Q_i\bigr)
    \]
    is well-defined (the right-hand side is symmetric in the \(Q_i\) and depends only on the unordered tuple) and is surjective. By Brill--Noether--Riemann--Roch, \(\sigma\) is birational onto its image: a generic divisor of degree \(g\) on \(C\) has \(h^0(\mathcal O_C(D)) = 1\), so the fibre \(\sigma^{-1}([\mathcal O_C(D)])\) is a point at generic divisors; the special fibres are projective spaces \(\mathbb P(h^0 - 1)\) over the locus where \(h^0 > 1\).
    \item[\textbf{(B.3)}] \emph{The Stein factorisation.} Because \(\sigma\) is a proper surjection with geometrically connected fibres (each fibre \(\mathbb P^{h^0-1}_k\) is geometrically irreducible), its Stein factorisation
    \[
      C^{(g)} \xrightarrow{\;\sigma_*\;} \Spec_{\Pic^g_{C/k}}\bigl(\sigma_* \mathcal O_{C^{(g)}}\bigr) \longrightarrow \Pic^g_{C/k}
    \]
    presents the second arrow as a finite morphism whose source is the intermediate scheme. Since the fibres of \(\sigma\) are connected, this intermediate scheme equals \(\Pic^g_{C/k}\) scheme-theoretically (the Stein factorisation is trivial on the second arrow when fibres are connected, by Stein's classical theorem). The middle scheme is therefore \(\Pic^g_{C/k}\) itself, which is smooth proper geometrically irreducible of dimension \(g\). Translating by \(-g P_0\) yields \(\Pic^0_{C/k}\), and we take \(J := \Pic^0_{C/k}\) as in Route A. The universal property is then established as in Sub-step A.4.
  \end{itemize}

  \paragraph{Genus-\(0\) sub-case (absorbed into the same witness).}
  When \(g(C) = 0\) the witness's underlying scheme is \(J = \Spec k\), and the universal property reduces to a rigidity statement.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item[\textbf{(C.1)}] \emph{Brauer--Severi identification.} A smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve \(C\) over \(k\) with \(g(C) = 0\) and a \(k\)-rational point \(P \in C(k)\) is isomorphic to \(\mathbb P^1_k\). This is the Brauer--Severi triviality criterion: a Brauer--Severi variety of dimension \(1\) is \(\mathbb P^1\) over its base if and only if it has a rational point. The genus-\(0\) identification on the curve side uses \(h^0(C, \mathcal O_C(P)) = 2\) (Riemann--Roch) to produce a degree-\(1\) morphism \(C \to \mathbb P^1_k\), which is then an isomorphism by degree-and-dimension.
    \item[\textbf{(C.2)}] \emph{Rigidity for morphisms from \(\mathbb P^1\) to an abelian variety.} We state the rigidity result over the algebraic closure \(\bar k\), since the protected signature of Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness} does not assume \(C(k) \neq \emptyset\) and the genus-\(0\) identification \(C \cong \mathbb P^1\) is only available after passing to \(\bar k\) (Brauer--Severi conics over \(\mathbb Q\) are the standard counterexamples to a \(k\)-side identification without a rational point). The conclusion over \(k\) --- equality of the given morphism with the constant morphism as \(k\)-schemes --- then follows by \emph{descent}: a morphism equality that holds after base change to \(\bar k\) descends to \(k\) along the faithfully-flat surjection \(\Spec \bar k \to \Spec k\) (Sub-step~C.2.f).
    \begin{itemize}
      \item[\textbf{(C.2.a)}] \emph{Statement (over \(\bar k\)).} Let \(A\) be a smooth proper geometrically irreducible group scheme over \(\bar k\), with identity element \(\eta_A \in A(\bar k)\). Let
      \[
        f \colon \mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \longrightarrow A
      \]
      be a morphism of \(\bar k\)-schemes such that \(f(p) = \eta_A\) for some closed point \(p \in \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}(\bar k)\). Then \(f\) factors as the composite \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \to \Spec \bar k \xrightarrow{\,\eta_A\,} A\); equivalently, \(f\) is the constant morphism at \(\eta_A\).
      \item[\textbf{(C.2.b)}] \emph{Reduction to the project's rigidity lemma.} Let \(c \colon \mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \to A\) denote the constant morphism at \(\eta_A\) --- the unique \(\bar k\)-morphism factoring through the closed-point section \(\eta_A \colon \Spec \bar k \hookrightarrow A\). The conclusion \(f = c\) follows from Theorem~\ref{thm:GrpObj_eq_of_eqOnOpen} applied to \(g_1 := f\), \(g_2 := c\), \(X := \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\), \(Y := A\), once we produce a non-empty open \(U \subseteq \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) on which \(U.\iota \circ f = U.\iota \circ c\) as scheme morphisms. (After the iter-125 refactor, the cited theorem is stated at the scheme level: source reduced and geometrically irreducible, target separated; the source-side group-object decoration that the earlier formulation carried has been dropped. \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) satisfies the refactored hypotheses trivially --- it is smooth, hence reduced; it is geometrically irreducible over \(\bar k\); and \(A\) is proper hence separated over \(\bar k\).) The reduction therefore amounts to: produce a non-empty open \(U \subseteq \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) on which \(f\) and \(c\) agree as scheme morphisms; we will in fact take \(U = \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\).
      \item[\textbf{(C.2.c)}] \emph{Image dimension argument.} The morphism \(f\) goes from a proper geometrically integral \(\bar k\)-scheme of dimension \(1\) to a smooth proper \(\bar k\)-scheme of dimension \(g := \dim_{\bar k} A\). The set-theoretic image \(f(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}) \subseteq A\) is an irreducible closed subset of \(A\) (the continuous image of an irreducible space under a proper map is irreducible and closed) of dimension at most \(1\). If \(g = 0\) then \(A = \Spec \bar k\) and the conclusion is vacuous: the unique \(\bar k\)-morphism to \(\Spec \bar k\) is the constant morphism. Henceforth assume \(g \geq 1\), in which case the image is either a single point (the desired conclusion) or a one-dimensional irreducible proper closed subscheme of \(A\) --- an irreducible rational curve in \(A\).
      \item[\textbf{(C.2.d)}] \emph{Key classical input: morphisms from \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) to an abelian variety are constant.} The remaining case --- image of dimension exactly \(1\) --- is ruled out by the classical fact that, for an abelian variety \(A\) of positive dimension over an algebraically closed field \(\bar k\), every morphism \(f \colon \mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \to A\) is constant. The project ranks three proof routes for this fact; route~(c) is \textbf{committed}, with (b) and (a) as fallbacks.
      \begin{itemize}
        \item[\textbf{(c) [committed; characteristic-free]}] \emph{Milne \S I.3 rigidity chain (cube-free).} Over \(\bar k\) a smooth proper geometrically irreducible genus-\(0\) curve is isomorphic to \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) (\cref{thm:genus_zero_curve_iso_p1}), and every morphism \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \to A\) to an abelian variety is constant (\cref{prop:morphism_P1_to_AV_constant}); a fortiori the morphism \(f\) is constant. The constancy of \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \to A\) is \emph{not} supplied by the theorem of the cube. The base case is the \(\mathbb G_a/\mathbb G_m\) group-law incompatibility (Milne Proposition~3.9, \cref{lem:hom_from_Ga_trivial}): the additive-defect map \(\psi(x,y) = f(x+y) - f(x) - f(y)\) on \(\mathbb G_a \times \mathbb G_a\) is extended to the complete surface \(\mathbb P^1 \times \mathbb P^1\) (rational-map extension \cref{thm:rational_map_to_av_extends}, Milne Thm~3.2/3.4) and then collapsed by the \emph{Rigidity Lemma} (\cref{thm:rigidity_lemma}, Mumford Form~I --- an elementary closed-map argument), forcing \(f|_{\mathbb G_a}\) to be an additive homomorphism; the same argument on \(\mathbb G_m\) produces a multiplicative relation incompatible with it unless \(f\) is constant. The additivity Corollary~1.5 (\cref{lem:hom_additivity_over_product}) packages the same collapse over a product. Combining with the genus-\(0\) classification yields the headline \cref{prop:rigidity_genus0_curve_to_AV}. This chain uses \emph{neither} the theorem of the cube, \emph{nor} \(H^0(C, \Omega_C) = 0\) / Serre duality, \emph{nor} the Picard representability engine, and is valid in arbitrary characteristic; it is developed in \cref{chap:AbelianVarietyRigidity}. It is the committed route for the genus-\(0\) keystone; the over-\(\bar k\) declaration \cref{thm:rigidity_over_kbar} of \cref{chap:RigidityKbar} (which still carries \([\mathtt{CharZero}]\)) is the fallback-(a) artifact, not consumed here.
        \item[\textbf{(b) [fallback]}] \emph{Via the dual abelian variety.} Pullback of line bundles along \(f\) induces a homomorphism of group schemes \(f^{\vee} \colon A^{\vee} \to \Pic^0(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k})\), where \(A^{\vee}\) is the dual abelian variety (the moduli space of degree-zero line bundles on \(A\)). The Picard group of \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) has trivial degree-zero component, \(\Pic^0(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}) = 0\), because every line bundle on \(\mathbb P^1\) of degree \(0\) is isomorphic to \(\mathcal O_{\mathbb P^1}\) (Hartshorne, \emph{Algebraic Geometry}, II.6 and II.7). Hence \(f^{\vee} = 0\). By autoduality of abelian varieties (\(A^{\vee\vee} = A\) canonically, double duality of the Poincar\'e line bundle), the zero of \(f^{\vee}\) implies that \(f\) itself is constant. This route is free once Route~A's \(\Pic^0\) engine exists, but it \emph{couples} the genus-\(0\) rigidity to the Route~A representability monster, which the committed route~(c) deliberately avoids.
        \item[\textbf{(a) [fallback; off the critical path; carries a characteristic-\(p\) gap]}] \emph{Via the cotangent bundle (the differential \(df = 0\) route).} The cotangent sheaf \(\Omega_{A/\bar k}\) of an abelian variety is a trivial \(\mathcal O_A\)-module: the tangent bundle of a group scheme over a field is generated globally by translates of the Lie algebra at the identity, hence is trivial, hence so is its dual. Pulling back along \(f\) yields a morphism of locally free sheaves \(f^{*} \Omega_{A/\bar k} \to \Omega_{\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}/\bar k}\), whose global sections form a \(\bar k\)-linear map
        \[
          H^0\bigl(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k},\, f^{*}\Omega_{A/\bar k}\bigr) \longrightarrow H^0\bigl(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k},\, \Omega_{\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}/\bar k}\bigr) = H^0\bigl(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k},\, \mathcal O_{\mathbb P^1}(-2)\bigr) = 0.
        \]
        Triviality of \(\Omega_{A/\bar k}\) means the left-hand side is a non-zero \(\bar k\)-vector space (of dimension \(g\)); the only way the map can land in zero is for it to be the zero map at every fibre, i.e.\ for the differential \(df\) to vanish identically on \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\). Smoothness of \(A\) over \(\bar k\) together with the vanishing of \(df\) forces \(f\) to be a constant morphism on the geometric points and hence (by reduced-source / separated-target, see C.2.e) as a scheme morphism. This is the \emph{differential} route: it requires the Serre-duality-flavoured vanishing \(H^0(\mathbb P^1, \Omega) = 0\) together with the cotangent-triviality of \(\Omega_A\), is retained in the tree via the closed chart-algebra envelope (which supplies only the converse implication \(df = 0 \Rightarrow f\) constant), and carries a characteristic-\(p\) gap. It is \emph{not} the committed route.
      \end{itemize}
      Each route yields: \(f\) is constant. The pointed condition \(f(p) = \eta_A\) then identifies the constant value, so \(f = c\) on underlying topological points.
      \item[\textbf{(C.2.e)}] \emph{Promoting set-level equality to scheme equality.} The set-level equality \(f = c\) on underlying topological points (established in C.2.d) promotes to scheme-morphism equality on any non-empty open \(U \subseteq \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) via the standard reduced-source / separated-target argument: \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) is reduced (smooth implies regular, regular implies reduced) and \(A\) is separated over \(\bar k\) (proper implies separated), so a \(\bar k\)-morphism \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \to A\) is determined by its values on the underlying topological space. In particular, \(f|_U = c|_U\) as scheme morphisms for the open \(U := \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) itself, and the reduction of C.2.b gives \(f = c\) as scheme morphisms.
      \item[\textbf{(C.2.f)}] \emph{Descent of morphism equality from \(\bar k\) to \(k\).} The rigidity conclusion of Sub-step~C.2.d is proved over the algebraic closure \(\bar k = \mathrm{AlgebraicClosure}\,k\), where the characteristic-free keystone \cref{prop:rigidity_genus0_curve_to_AV} (\cref{chap:AbelianVarietyRigidity}) carries the hypothesis \([\mathtt{IsAlgClosed}\ \bar k]\) and, being characteristic-free, no \([\mathtt{CharZero}]\). To obtain the corresponding equality of morphisms over the base field \(k\), we descend along the base change \(\Spec \bar k \to \Spec k\).
      Concretely, let \(f, c \colon C \to A\) be two \(k\)-morphisms (in the application \(f\) is the given map and \(c\) the constant map at \(\eta_A\)). Base-change along \(\Spec \bar k \to \Spec k\) produces \(f_{\bar k}, c_{\bar k} \colon C_{\bar k} \to A_{\bar k}\) together with the projections \(p \colon C_{\bar k} = C \times_k \bar k \to C\) and \(q \colon A_{\bar k} \to A\), fitting the base-change squares \(q \circ f_{\bar k} = f \circ p\) and \(q \circ c_{\bar k} = c \circ p\). Suppose \(f_{\bar k} = c_{\bar k}\) (this is what \cref{prop:rigidity_genus0_curve_to_AV} delivers over \(\bar k\)). Then \(f \circ p = q \circ f_{\bar k} = q \circ c_{\bar k} = c \circ p\), so \(f\) and \(c\) agree after pre-composition with \(p\).
      It remains to right-cancel \(p\). The morphism \(\Spec \bar k \to \Spec k\) is faithfully flat (a field extension is a faithfully flat ring map) and surjective; both properties are stable under base change, so the projection \(p \colon C_{\bar k} \to C\) is faithfully flat and surjective. By \texttt{AlgebraicGeometry.Flat.epi\_of\_flat\_of\_surjective}, \(p\) is therefore an epimorphism of schemes, and right-cancelling it in \(f \circ p = c \circ p\) gives \(f = c\) over \(k\). \emph{This closing epi-cancellation is the only genuinely \(\sim 2\)-line part.} The descent as a whole is \textbf{not} a \(\sim 2\)-line step but a multi-iteration base-change sub-build: neither this project nor Mathlib provides a base-change functor \(\mathrm{Over}\,(\Spec k) \to \mathrm{Over}\,(\Spec \bar k)\), so even producing \(f_{\bar k}, c_{\bar k}\) and the base-change squares \(q \circ f_{\bar k} = f \circ p\) above first requires (a) that functor; (b) transfer of every relevant instance along it (\(\mathtt{SmoothOfRelativeDimension}\,1\), \(\mathtt{IsProper}\), \(\mathtt{GeometricallyIrreducible}\), \(\mathtt{GrpObj}\)); (c) stability of \(\genus = 0\) under the base change \(\Spec \bar k \to \Spec k\) for a geometrically irreducible curve; and (d) the base-change-square commutativity identities. All four must be in place before the closing epi-cancellation applies; only that final cancellation is short, while the surrounding base-change infrastructure is the bulk of the cost.
      \item[\textbf{(C.2.g)}] \emph{Mathlib gap statement.} The keystone fact of Sub-step~C.2.d --- that every morphism from a smooth proper geometrically irreducible genus-\(0\) curve over an algebraically closed field \(\bar k\) to a positive-dimensional smooth proper geometrically irreducible group scheme over \(\bar k\) is constant --- is \emph{not} present in current Mathlib (snapshot \texttt{b80f227}) in any form usable here. The project records the committed, characteristic-free form as the named declaration \cref{prop:rigidity_genus0_curve_to_AV} of \cref{chap:AbelianVarietyRigidity} (carrying \([\mathtt{IsAlgClosed}\ \bar k]\) and \emph{no} \([\mathtt{CharZero}]\) hypothesis); the older over-\(\bar k\) declaration \cref{thm:rigidity_over_kbar} of \cref{chap:RigidityKbar}, whose base field still carries both \([\mathtt{IsAlgClosed}\ \bar k]\) and \([\mathtt{CharZero}\ \bar k]\), is retained only as the fallback-(a) artifact. The \textbf{committed} proof route is route~(c) of C.2.d: the Milne \S I.3 rigidity chain (cube-free) (\cref{chap:AbelianVarietyRigidity}, headline \cref{prop:morphism_P1_to_AV_constant} feeding \cref{prop:rigidity_genus0_curve_to_AV}), which is characteristic-free and uses neither the theorem of the cube, nor Serre duality, nor the Picard engine. The differential decomposition into the shared cotangent-vanishing pile of \cref{sec:RigidityKbar_shared_pile} pieces (i)--(iii) --- (i) cotangent triviality of a group scheme over \(\bar k\) via the functorial shear iso; (ii) scheme-level \(\mathrm d f = 0 \Rightarrow\) \(f\) factors through \(\Spec \bar k\) via the \texttt{Differential.ContainConstants} typeclass plus the iter-125-refactored \texttt{Scheme.Over.ext\_of\_eqOnOpen}; (iii) characteristic-\(p\) handling via the intrinsic absolute Frobenius \(F_X\) --- is the \textbf{fallback} route~(a), off the critical path but retained in the tree (each piece an upstream Mathlib gap-fill in its own right; see \cref{chap:RigidityKbar}). When \(g = \dim A = 0\) the statement is vacuous and the declaration's conclusion is automatic. The reduction step C.2.b reuses the project's already-formalised Theorem~\ref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness} (after the iter-125 refactor, this is \texttt{AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.Over.ext\_of\_eqOnOpen}, stated at the scheme level: source reduced and geometrically irreducible, target separated); the promotion step C.2.e uses standard reduced-source / separated-target lemmas, partially in Mathlib. The descent step C.2.f --- from \(\bar k\) back to the base field \(k\) --- has a genuinely short \emph{final} move (the epi-cancellation via \texttt{AlgebraicGeometry.Flat.epi\_of\_flat\_of\_surjective}, already in Mathlib in \texttt{Mathlib.AlgebraicGeometry.Morphisms.Flat}), but the descent as a whole is \emph{not} a \(\sim 2\)-line step: it is a multi-iteration base-change sub-build, because neither this project nor Mathlib provides a base-change functor \(\mathrm{Over}\,(\Spec k) \to \mathrm{Over}\,(\Spec \bar k)\), and the instance transfer, genus-stability, and base-change-square identities (C.2.f items (a)--(d)) must all be built before the closing epi-cancellation applies.
    \end{itemize}
    \item[\textbf{(C.3)}] \emph{The trivial witness.} Take \(J := \Spec k\), the terminal \(k\)-scheme. The group-object structure on \(\Spec k\) is trivial; it is smooth proper geometrically irreducible of relative dimension \(0 = g(C)\). The unique morphism \(\iota_P \colon C \to \Spec k\) sends \(P\) to the (unique) point. By Sub-step C.2 every \(f \colon C \to A\) with \(f(P) = \eta_A\) is constant at \(\eta_A\), hence factors uniquely through the unique morphism \(g \colon \Spec k \to A\) sending the point to \(\eta_A\); uniqueness of \(g\) follows because \(\Spec k \to A\) is determined by its value on the unique \(k\)-rational point of \(\Spec k\).
  \end{itemize}

\subsection{Route (c): rigidity via Milne \S I.3 (cube-free; committed genus-\(0\) proof route)}
\label{sec:av_rigidity_route_c}
The committed genus-\(0\) route~(c) --- the characteristic-free abelian-variety rigidity stack --- is now developed in its own chapter, \cref{chap:AbelianVarietyRigidity}, which sits \emph{upstream} of this chapter in the import graph: it covers \texttt{AlgebraicJacobian/AbelianVarietyRigidity.lean}, which imports \texttt{AlgebraicJacobian.Genus} and is imported by \texttt{AlgebraicJacobian.Jacobian}. This relocation is precisely what breaks the former \texttt{RigidityKbar}\,\(\to\)\,\texttt{Rigidity}\,\(\to\)\,\texttt{Jacobian} import cycle, so that \cref{def:genusZeroWitness} can consume the keystone directly. The genus-\(0\) arm consumes that chapter's headline \cref{prop:rigidity_genus0_curve_to_AV} --- the characteristic-free replacement (\emph{no} \([\mathtt{CharZero}]\) hypothesis) for the over-\(\bar k\) declaration \cref{thm:rigidity_over_kbar} of \cref{chap:RigidityKbar}, which remains in tree as the fallback-(a) artifact and still carries \([\mathtt{CharZero}]\).
The chain of \cref{chap:AbelianVarietyRigidity} is \textbf{cube-free}: the theorem of the cube is \emph{not} on the genus-\(0\) path (it enters Milne's theory only later, for projectivity, the seesaw principle, dual abelian varieties, and the Poincar\'e sheaf). It has four links, all resting on Milne \S I.3. The \emph{Rigidity Lemma} (\cref{thm:rigidity_lemma}, Mumford Form~I) is an elementary properness/closed-map argument --- cohomology-free --- and supplies the collapse of a complete-factor map. From it, the \emph{additive decomposition} Corollary~1.5 (\cref{lem:hom_additivity_over_product}) and \emph{Corollary~1.2} that a pointed regular map of abelian varieties is a homomorphism follow directly. The single-curve base case --- that every morphism \(\mathbb P^1_{\bar k} \to A\) is constant --- is supplied \emph{not} by the cube but by the \(\mathbb G_a/\mathbb G_m\) group-law incompatibility (Milne Proposition~3.9, \cref{lem:hom_from_Ga_trivial}): the additive-defect map \(\psi(x,y) = f(x+y) - f(x) - f(y)\), defined a priori only on the affine surface \(\mathbb G_a \times \mathbb G_a\), is extended to the complete surface \(\mathbb P^1 \times \mathbb P^1\) by the rational-map-extension Theorem~3.2/3.4 (\cref{thm:rational_map_to_av_extends}) and then collapsed by the Rigidity Lemma, forcing \(f|_{\mathbb G_a}\) to be an additive homomorphism; the parallel relation \(f(xy) = f(x) + f(y) + c\) on \(\mathbb G_m\) is incompatible with it unless \(f\) is constant. This gives \cref{prop:morphism_P1_to_AV_constant}; composing with the genus-\(0\) classification \(C \cong \mathbb P^1_{\bar k}\) over \(\bar k\) (\cref{thm:genus_zero_curve_iso_p1}) yields \cref{prop:rigidity_genus0_curve_to_AV}. This chain uses \emph{neither} the theorem of the cube, \emph{nor} \(H^0(C, \Omega_C) = 0\) / Serre duality, \emph{nor} the FGA \(\Pic\) representability engine, so the genus-\(0\) arm is fully \textbf{decoupled} from Route~A; the differential / Serre-duality argument survives only as the off-critical-path fallback~(a) of \cref{chap:RigidityKbar}. The route's \textbf{riskiest sub-build} is the surface rational-map-extension step (Milne Lemma~3.3, pure-codimension-\(1\) indeterminacy on \(\mathbb P^1 \times \mathbb P^1\), for which Mathlib has no Weil-divisor theory; see \cref{chap:AbelianVarietyRigidity} \cref{rmk:thm32_codim1_mathlib_gap}), which is genuinely on the genus-\(0\) critical path and cannot be deferred to Route~A.
\begin{lemma}

\leanok
[Geometric irreducibility of the identity on \(\Spec k\)]
  \label{lem:geometricallyIrreducible_id_Spec}
  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.geometricallyIrreducible_id_Spec}
  For a field \(k\), the identity morphism \(\mathrm{id}_{\Spec k} \colon \Spec k \to \Spec k\) is geometrically irreducible: for every field extension \(k \to K\), the corresponding geometric fibre is irreducible. \begin{theorem}\leanok
[Genus-\(0\) Albanese witness]
  \label{def:genusZeroWitness}
  \uses{def:JacobianWitness, def:IsAlbanese, def:genus, prop:rigidity_genus0_curve_to_AV, lem:geometricallyIrreducible_id_Spec}

  \lean{AlgebraicGeometry.genusZeroWitness}
  Let \(C \in \mathrm{Over}\,(\Spec k)\) be a smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve over \(k\) with \(\genus(C) = 0\). Then there exists a \(\mathtt{JacobianWitness}\,C\) whose underlying scheme is \(\Spec k\) (the terminal object of \(\mathrm{Over}\,(\Spec k)\)), with the trivial group-object structure, smoothness of relative dimension \(0\), properness, and geometric irreducibility, and whose \(\mathtt{isAlbaneseFor}\) field encodes the Albanese universal property for every \(k\)-rational marked point \(P : \mathbf{1} \to C\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  We construct the seven fields of \cref{def:JacobianWitness}.
  \paragraph{Underlying scheme.} Take \(J := \mathbf{1}\), the terminal object of \(\mathrm{Over}\,(\Spec k)\), which on underlying schemes is \(\Spec k\) viewed as a \(k\)-scheme via the identity structure morphism.
  \paragraph{Group-object structure, properness, smoothness, geometric irreducibility.} The terminal object \(\mathbf{1}\) of any cartesian-monoidal category carries the (unique) trivial group-object structure: multiplication and inverse are the unique morphisms to \(\mathbf{1}\), and the identity element is the identity morphism. The structure morphism \(\mathbf{1}.\mathtt{hom} = \mathrm{id}_{\Spec k}\) is proper (the identity is a closed immersion, in particular proper), smooth (the identity is unramified and flat, hence smooth), and geometrically irreducible. The geometric-irreducibility instance is the project-local helper \texttt{AlgebraicGeometry.geometricallyIrreducible\_id\_Spec} (\texttt{AlgebraicJacobian/Jacobian.lean:134}--\texttt{140}), which derives geometric irreducibility of the identity on \(\Spec k\) from the fact that, on the geometric fibre \(\Spec K\) over any field extension \(k \to K\), the second projection of the pullback square is an isomorphism, and \(\Spec K\) is irreducible.
  \paragraph{Smoothness of relative dimension \(\genus\,C\).} The smoothness witness \(\mathtt{smoothGenus}\) is required at relative dimension \(\genus\,C\). The hypothesis \(\genus\,C = 0\) rewrites the target relative dimension to \(0\), after which the goal reduces to \(\mathtt{SmoothOfRelativeDimension}\ 0\ (\mathrm{id}_{\Spec k})\). This is a Mathlib instance: the identity morphism on any base is smooth of relative dimension \(0\).
  \paragraph{The \texttt{isAlbaneseFor} field.} For every \(k\)-rational marked point \(P : \mathbf{1} \to C\), we exhibit an \cref{def:IsAlbanese} term for the triple \((C, P, J)\).
  The universal pointed morphism is the unique terminal map \(\alpha := \mathtt{toUnit}\,C \colon C \to \mathbf{1}\) (the structure morphism of \(C\) as a \(k\)-scheme, factored through the terminal object). The pointed condition \(P \circ \alpha = \eta_J = \mathrm{id}_{\mathbf{1}}\) holds automatically: both sides are morphisms \(\mathbf{1} \to \mathbf{1}\) in \(\mathrm{Over}\,(\Spec k)\), and the terminal object has a unique endomorphism.
  For the universal factorisation: given a smooth proper geometrically irreducible group scheme \(A\) over \(k\) with identity element \(\eta_A \colon \mathbf{1} \to A\) and a morphism \(f \colon C \to A\) with \(P \circ f = \eta_A\), we must exhibit a unique morphism \(g \colon J \to A\) such that \(f = \alpha \circ g\). \emph{Existence}: take \(g := \eta_A \colon \mathbf{1} \to A\). Then \(\alpha \circ g = \mathtt{toUnit}\,C \circ \eta_A\), which equals \(f\) precisely when \(f\) is the constant morphism at \(\eta_A\). We obtain this equality by descent from the algebraic closure \(\bar k = \mathrm{AlgebraicClosure}\,k\). Base-change the data along \(\Spec \bar k \to \Spec k\): the curve \(C_{\bar k}\) is again smooth proper geometrically irreducible of genus \(0\) (genus is stable under base change for a geometrically irreducible curve), the group scheme \(A_{\bar k}\) is again smooth proper geometrically irreducible, the base-changed point \(P_{\bar k}\) is a \(\bar k\)-rational point, and \(f_{\bar k}\) still kills it: \(f_{\bar k}(P_{\bar k}) = \eta_{A_{\bar k}}\). By \cref{prop:rigidity_genus0_curve_to_AV} (the project's characteristic-free genus-\(0\) rigidity keystone of \cref{chap:AbelianVarietyRigidity}, carrying \([\mathtt{IsAlgClosed}\ \bar k]\) and \emph{no} \([\mathtt{CharZero}]\) hypothesis; it lives in the upstream \texttt{AbelianVarietyRigidity.lean}, imported by \texttt{Jacobian.lean}, so consuming it here introduces no import cycle), \(f_{\bar k}\) equals the constant morphism \(\mathtt{toUnit}\,C_{\bar k} \circ \eta_{A_{\bar k}}\) over \(\bar k\). Finally we descend this morphism equality back to \(k\): the projection \(p \colon C_{\bar k} \to C\) is the base change of the faithfully-flat surjection \(\Spec \bar k \to \Spec k\), hence faithfully flat and surjective, hence an epimorphism of schemes by \texttt{AlgebraicGeometry.Flat.epi\_of\_flat\_of\_surjective}; right-cancelling \(p\) against the base-change-square identities yields \(f = \mathtt{toUnit}\,C \circ \eta_A\) as \(k\)-scheme morphisms (Sub-step~C.2.f of \cref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}). \emph{Uniqueness}: if \(g_1, g_2 \colon \mathbf{1} \to A\) both satisfy \(f = \alpha \circ g_i\) with \(\alpha = \mathtt{toUnit}\,C\), then \(\alpha \circ g_1 = \alpha \circ g_2\), and we right-cancel \(\alpha\). This is the \emph{same} epi-cancellation as in the Existence paragraph: the underlying scheme morphism of \(\alpha\) is \(C.\mathtt{hom} \colon C \to \Spec k\) (\texttt{Over.toUnit\_left}), which is faithfully flat and surjective (a smooth proper geometrically irreducible curve is faithfully flat and surjective over its base field), hence an epimorphism of schemes by \texttt{AlgebraicGeometry.Flat.epi\_of\_flat\_of\_surjective}; therefore \(\alpha\) is an epimorphism in \(\mathrm{Over}\,(\Spec k)\) by \texttt{Over.epi\_of\_epi\_left}, and \texttt{cancel\_epi} gives \(g_1 = g_2\). Uniqueness does \emph{not} follow from terminality of \(\mathbf{1}\): morphisms \emph{out} of a terminal object are global points and are not unique in general (terminality gives uniqueness only of morphisms \emph{into} \(\mathbf{1}\)).
  \paragraph{Vacuity for the \(C(k) = \emptyset\) branch.} When no \(k\)-rational point \(P : \mathbf{1} \to C\) exists (Brauer--Severi conics over \(\mathbb Q\) are the standard counterexample), the universal quantifier \(\forall (P : \mathbf{1} \to C), \mathtt{IsAlbanese}\,C\,P\,J\) is vacuously satisfied at the Lean type level: Lean's \(\forall\) over an empty type is trivially true. The construction handles this branch implicitly without any explicit case split.
  \paragraph{Body closure status.} The Lean body of \texttt{AlgebraicGeometry.genusZeroWitness} (in \texttt{AlgebraicJacobian/Jacobian.lean}) is currently \texttt{sorry}; the closure is gated on the body of the upstream keystone \cref{prop:rigidity_genus0_curve_to_AV} (M2.a; in \texttt{AlgebraicJacobian/AbelianVarietyRigidity.lean}, imported by \texttt{Jacobian.lean}), whose \textbf{committed} proof route is route~(c) --- the characteristic-free Milne \S I.3 rigidity chain (cube-free) of \cref{chap:AbelianVarietyRigidity}, which is decoupled from the theorem of the cube, Route~A representability, and Serre duality. (The over-\(\bar k\) declaration \cref{thm:rigidity_over_kbar} of \cref{chap:RigidityKbar} is the retained fallback-(a) artifact, not the keystone consumed here.) The differential decomposition into pieces (i)+(ii)+(iii) of the shared cotangent-vanishing pile (\cref{sec:RigidityKbar_shared_pile}) is the retained \emph{fallback} route~(a), off the critical path. (The chart-algebra envelope of the fallback supplies only the converse implication \(df = 0 \Rightarrow\) constant, and carries a characteristic-\(p\) gap; the committed route~(c) avoids that gap entirely.)
\end{proof}

\section{Implementation route via the Albanese functor}
Because the full Picard-scheme representability theorem is not available in current Mathlib, the formal construction proceeds in two layers:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Layer I --- direct definition.} The Jacobian of \(C\) is defined uniformly as the underlying scheme of the Albanese witness provided by \cref{thm:nonempty_jacobianWitness}, carrying the four properties of \cref{thm:Jacobian_grpObj,thm:Jacobian_smooth_genus,thm:Jacobian_proper,thm:Jacobian_geomIrred} (each obtained by projection from the witness).
