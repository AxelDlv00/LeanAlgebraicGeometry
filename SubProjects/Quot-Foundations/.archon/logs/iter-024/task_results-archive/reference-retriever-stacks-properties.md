### Tag 01B5 — Definition: finite-type O_X-module
- **Label**: `modules-definition-finite-type` in `modules.tex`
- **Location**: modules.tex, lines 809–817
- **Verbatim statement** (from downloaded file):
  ```
  \begin{definition}
  \label{definition-finite-type}
  Let $(X, \mathcal{O}_X)$ be a ringed space.
  Let $\mathcal{F}$ be a sheaf of $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules.
  We say that $\mathcal{F}$ is of {\it finite type}
  if for every $x \in X$ there exists an open
  neighbourhood $U$ such that $\mathcal{F}|_U$
  is generated by finitely many sections.
  \end{definition}
  ```
- Note: This definition is in `modules.tex`, referenced from `properties.tex` at line 2082 as `Modules, Definition \ref{modules-definition-finite-type}`.

### Tag 01PB — Lemma: M̃ is finite-type iff M is finite R-module  ← PRIMARY TARGET
- **Label**: `properties-lemma-finite-type-module`
- **Location**: `references/stacks-properties.tex`, lines 2092–2110
- **Verbatim statement** (from downloaded file):
  ```
  \begin{lemma}
  \label{lemma-finite-type-module}
  Let $X = \Spec(R)$ be an affine scheme.
  The quasi-coherent sheaf of $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules
  $\widetilde M$ is a finite type $\mathcal{O}_X$-module
  if and only if $M$ is a finite $R$-module.
  \end{lemma}
  ```
- **Proof** (lines 2100–2110): Assumes finite-type, extracts a standard open covering D(f_i), reduces to M_{f_i} finitely generated, applies Algebra, Lemma `algebra-lemma-cover`. Conversely trivial.
