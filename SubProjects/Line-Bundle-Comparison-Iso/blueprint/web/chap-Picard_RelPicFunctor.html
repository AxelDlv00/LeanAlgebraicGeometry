2 The relative Picard functor and its étale sheafification

2.1 Setup and motivation

Fix a base field \(k\) and a smooth proper geometrically integral curve \(C\) over \(k\). As in 1, write \(\pi _T : C \times _k T \to T\) for the projection of a relative curve over a \(k\)-scheme \(T\) and \(\pi _T^* : \operatorname{Pic}(T) \to \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T)\) for the induced pullback of isomorphism classes of line bundles (4).

The set-valued relative Picard presheaf

\[ \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T) \; \; =\; \; \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T) \, / \, \pi _T^*\operatorname{Pic}(T) \]

of 5 was set up forgetting the abelian-group structure inherited from tensor product. This chapter restores that structure and then sheafifies. The first half (2.2 and 2.3) verifies that the quotient is canonically an abelian group and the functoriality of pullback_natural respects that group structure; the result is a group-valued presheaf \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k} : (\operatorname{Sch}/k)^{op} \to \mathrm{Ab}\) on the category of \(k\)-schemes. The second half (2.4) takes the étale sheafification \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{(C/k)\mathrm{\acute{e}t}} := (\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k})^\sim \) in the étale topology on \((\operatorname{Sch}/k)^{op}\), and verifies that sheafification preserves the abelian-group target. The étale-sheafified functor is the object represented by the Picard scheme in the FGA Picard-representability chapter (parent scope).

2.2 Group structure on the relative Picard quotient

The Picard group \(\operatorname{Pic}(X)\) of any scheme is canonically an abelian group under tensor product of line bundles (Stacks tag 01CR; the inverse of \([\mathcal{L}]\) is \([\mathcal{L}^{-1}] = [\mathcal{H}om_{\mathcal{O}_X}(\mathcal{L}, \mathcal{O}_X)]\)). The pullback map \(\pi _T^* : \operatorname{Pic}(T) \to \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T)\) of 4 respects this structure: pullback of \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-modules sends \(\mathcal{O}_T\) to \(\mathcal{O}_{C \times _k T}\) and is multiplicative on tensor products.

[Group structure descends to the relative Picard quotient]

Source: [Kleiman], “The Picard scheme”, §2, Defs. df:aPf + df:Pfs (the Picard functors are abelian-group-valued, and the relative one is a quotient of abelian groups; cf. Stacks tag 01CR for the abelian-group structure on \(\operatorname{Pic}\)). Let \(C/k\) be a smooth proper geometrically integral curve and let \(T\) be a \(k\)-scheme. The Picard group \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T)\) is an abelian group under tensor product of line bundles, and the pullback map

\[ \pi _T^* \; \colon \; \operatorname{Pic}(T) \; \longrightarrow \; \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T) \]

of 4 is a group homomorphism. Its image

\[ H_T \; \; :=\; \; \pi _T^* \operatorname{Pic}(T) \]

is therefore a subgroup of \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T)\), and the quotient set of 5

\[ \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T) \; \; =\; \; \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T) \, / \, H_T \]

inherits a canonical abelian-group structure under which the quotient map \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T) \twoheadrightarrow \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T)\) is a surjective homomorphism with kernel exactly \(H_T\).

Proof ▶

The construction proceeds in four steps over the locally-trivial carrier

\[ \mathrm{OnProduct}(\pi _C, \pi _T) \; =\; \bigl\{ \, M : \text{a module on } C \times _k T \; \bigm |\; M \text{ is locally trivial} \, \bigr\} \]

of 1, on whose isomorphism classes the relative Picard quotient is taken. It is authored in parallel against two inputs being built concurrently: the locally-trivial pullback–tensor comparison isomorphism 47 (consumed in Step 2 to make \(\pi _T^*\) additive) and the tensor inverse 21 (consumed in Step 1 for group closure); both are themselves deferred targets.

Step 1 (abelian group on the carrier). On isomorphism classes of the carrier \(\mathrm{OnProduct}(\pi _C, \pi _T)\), addition is the descent of the scheme-level tensor product of line bundles,

\[ [L] + [L'] \; :=\; [\, L \otimes _{\mathcal{O}_{C \times _k T}} L' \, ], \]

where \(L \otimes L'\) is the locally-trivial descent of the scheme-level tensor product (22); the sum lands again in the carrier because the tensor product of two locally-trivial modules is locally trivial (13). The zero element is the class \([\mathcal{O}_{C \times _k T}]\) of the structure sheaf, the monoidal unit, which is locally trivial. Associativity, commutativity, and left/right unitality of \(+\) descend from the scheme-level coherence isomorphisms — the associator (18), the braiding (20), and the unitors (19) — exactly as for the absolute Picard group on tensor-invertible isomorphism classes (27). The additive inverse is

\[ -[L] \; :=\; [\, L^{\mathrm{inv}} \, ], \]

where \(L^{\mathrm{inv}}\) is the witness supplied by 21: it returns a locally-trivial module together with an isomorphism \(L \otimes L^{\mathrm{inv}} \cong \mathcal{O}_{C \times _k T}\), so the inverse stays inside the loc-triv carrier and the group is closed under negation.

Step 2 (the pullback homomorphism \(\pi _T^*\)). Pullback along the projection \(\pi _T\) gives an additive map

\[ \pi _T^* \; \colon \; \operatorname{Pic}(T) \; \longrightarrow \; \mathrm{OnProduct}(\pi _C, \pi _T), \]

whose underlying assignment of line bundles is 4. It is a homomorphism of abelian groups: it sends the unit to the unit, since \(\pi _T^*\mathcal{O}_T \cong \mathcal{O}_{C \times _k T}\) by the unconditional pullback–unit isomorphism (34); and it is additive, since pullback commutes with tensor product on locally-trivial modules,

\[ \pi _T^*(N \otimes _{\mathcal{O}_T} N') \; \cong \; \pi _T^* N \otimes _{\mathcal{O}_{C \times _k T}} \pi _T^* N', \]

by the locally-trivial comparison isomorphism 47 specialised to the projection \(\pi _T\) on the \(\mathrm{OnProduct}\) carrier. This is the exact analogue, for the geometric pullback, of the additive monoid homomorphism on Picard groups induced by a ring map respecting tensor product, and is modelled on it field-for-field. Write \(H_T := \mathrm{im}(\pi _T^*)\) for its image, a subgroup of the carrier’s isomorphism-class group.

Step 3 (setoid reconciliation). The set-valued quotient of 5 is taken modulo the isomorphism-class equivalence that declares two line bundles related when their classes differ by an element of \(H_T\). This relation coincides with the left-coset relation of the subgroup \(H_T\) inside the abelian group of Steps 1–2: both encode

\[ L \sim L' \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad [\, L^{\mathrm{inv}} \otimes L' \, ] \in H_T . \]

Establishing this equivalence of relations is a concrete isomorphism-class argument over the carrier; it consumes the tensor inverse (Step 1) and the group law, but not the computational content of the comparison isomorphism.

Step 4 (transport). By Step 3 the quotient set underlying \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T)\) is in canonical bijection with the group quotient \(\mathrm{OnProduct}(\pi _C, \pi _T) / H_T\), which carries the canonical abelian-group structure of a quotient of an abelian group by a subgroup. Transporting this structure along the bijection of Step 3 endows \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T)\) with its abelian-group structure, under which the canonical surjection \(\operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T) \twoheadrightarrow \operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}(T)\) is a homomorphism with kernel exactly \(H_T\).

2.2.1 Helper constructions underlying the group law

The abelian-group instance of 6 is assembled from a handful of project-internal helper constructions on the locally-trivial carrier \(\mathrm{OnProduct}(\pi _C, \pi _T)\): the tensor-product operation that realises addition, the local triviality of the unit (the zero element), the uniqueness of tensor inverses (which makes negation well defined), and the two descended operations on isomorphism classes. Each is recorded here for the one-to-one Lean correspondence; all are proved directly in Lean.

2.3 The relative Picard presheaf as a group-valued presheaf

We now refine the set-valued functor of pullback_natural into a group-valued presheaf.

2.4 Étale sheafification

The presheaf \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}\) is generally not an étale (or even Zariski) sheaf. The standard obstruction (Kleiman §2, L1292–L1302): for a \(k\)-scheme \(T\) carrying a non-trivial line bundle whose pullback to \(C \times _k T\) becomes non-trivial after an open cover, the Zariski separated-presheaf condition fails. Therefore, to obtain a representable functor in the sense of the FGA Picard-representability chapter (parent scope), one must replace \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}\) by its associated étale sheaf. Kleiman §2 names this sheaf \(\operatorname{Pic}_{(X/S)\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}\) and singles it out as the functor that Kleiman’s main existence theorem in §4 represents.

At the current Lean state, only PicSharp.addCommGroup is realized as a live, sorry-free declaration. The remaining items below are the intended end-state interface and should be read as provisional scaffolding, blocked on the upstream D3′/DUAL comparison and the A-bridge, not as already-finished Lean code.

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.PicSharp.addCommGroup (6) is the abelian-group instance on the quotient set built in AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.RelPicPresheaf.preimage_subgroup (5). Mathlib’s QuotientAddGroup machinery on a normal subgroup of an abelian group is the backbone; the project-side work is to certify that \(\pi _T^*\) is a group homomorphism (one extra LinearMap.map_mul–style line on tensor product preservation).

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.PicSharp (rel_pic_sharp) is the group-valued presheaf \(T \mapsto \operatorname{Pic}(C \times _k T)/\pi _T^*\operatorname{Pic}(T)\). The Lean form is a functor \((\operatorname{Sch}/k)^{op} \to \mathrm{AddCommGroup}\) built from the data of 5 + 6.

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.PicSharp.functorial (rel_pic_sharp_functorial) packages the structure-map homomorphism property: the set map \(g^\sharp \) of pullback_natural is a group homomorphism. On the Lean side this is a one-step strengthening of the existing set-valued naturality proof.

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.PicSharp.presheaf (rel_pic_sharp_presheaf) wraps the previous three lemmas as a functor instance into \(\mathrm{AddCommGroup}\).

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.PicSharp.etSheaf (rel_pic_etale_sheafification) is the étale sheafification of PicSharp. The Lean form is CategoryTheory.presheafToSheaf applied to the big étale topology on Scheme with target category AddCommGrpCat. Lean API note. The big étale Grothendieck topology on the category of schemes is AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.etaleTopology, of type CategoryTheory.GrothendieckTopology AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme (module Mathlib.AlgebraicGeometry.Sites.Etale; an abbrev for the topology generated by the big étale pretopology Scheme.etalePretopology). The sheafification entry point with abelian-group target is CategoryTheory.presheafToSheaf AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.etaleTopology AddCommGrpCat, of type Functor (Schemeᵒᵖ \(\Rightarrow \) AddCommGrpCat) (Sheaf etaleTopology AddCommGrpCat) (module Mathlib.CategoryTheory.Sites.Sheafification); it is available under a CategoryTheory.HasWeakSheafify instance for the pair (etaleTopology, AddCommGrpCat). The slice \((\operatorname{Sch}/k)\) on the Lean side is captured by working over the global Scheme category and restricting along Over (Spec k) via Mathlib’s CategoryTheory.Sites.Over / GrothendieckTopology pullback construction (the relative Picard functor’s natural domain is the slice; Kleiman’s notation \((\operatorname{Sch}/S)^{op}\) corresponds to (Over (Spec k))ᵒᵖ). If the HasWeakSheafify instance for the pair (etaleTopology, AddCommGrpCat) is missing at the pinned Mathlib commit, the project-side fallback combines the concrete set-valued sheafification CategoryTheory.GrothendieckTopology.sheafify (module Mathlib.CategoryTheory.Sites.ConcreteSheafification) with the colimit-preservation of the forgetful functor \(\texttt{AddCommGrpCat} \to \mathbf{Set}\) and the plusPlusSheaf plus-construction on the underlying set-valued presheaf, transported back through the ConcreteCategory instance on AddCommGrpCat.

  • AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.PicSharp.etSheaf_group_structure (rel_pic_etale_sheaf_group_structure) packages the fact that the étale sheafification preserves the abelian-group target. On the Lean side it is the canonical Functor (Schemeᵒᵖ \(\Rightarrow \) AddCommGrpCat) obtained by applying CategoryTheory.presheafToSheaf AlgebraicGeometry.Scheme.etaleTopology AddCommGrpCat to PicSharp.presheaf and then forgetting from the sheaf category back to a presheaf-of-abelian-groups via (CategoryTheory.sheafToPresheaf etaleTopology AddCommGrpCat). The proof is structural: it identifies etSheaf (typed as a sheaf of sets via the concrete plus-construction CategoryTheory.GrothendieckTopology.sheafify) with the value of presheafToSheaf etaleTopology AddCommGrpCat PicSharp.presheaf under the forgetful functor, using CategoryTheory.GrothendieckTopology.sheafification_obj (the equality \(\texttt{J.sheafification D}.\mathrm{obj}\, P = \texttt{J.sheafify}\, P\)) and the fact that the forgetful functor \(\texttt{AddCommGrpCat} \to \mathbf{Set}\) preserves the multiequalizer / filtered-colimit data used by the plus-construction (plusPlusSheaf commutes with forget on a ConcreteCategory target).

The relationship with the FGA Picard-representability chapter (parent scope) is captured by the slogan: the FGA Picard scheme of A.2.c represents \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{(C/k)\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}\) (Kleiman §4, the main existence theorem: “…represents \(\operatorname{Pic}_{(X/S)\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}\)”), not \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{C/k}\) directly. The étale sheafification step of this chapter is precisely what makes the representability theorem in A.2.c well-posed.

  • Identity component \(\operatorname{Pic}^0_{C/k}\) (A.3) — the open subgroup scheme cut out by Hilbert polynomials, treated in a downstream chapter.

  • Albanese universal property (A.4) — consumes the full Route A stack.

  • Group-scheme structure on the representing object — once \(\operatorname{Pic}^\sharp _{(C/k)\mathrm{\acute{e}t}}\) is representable (A.2.c), its representing scheme inherits a \(k\)-group-scheme structure from the abelian-group-valued target via the Yoneda embedding; that refinement is part of A.2.c, not of this chapter.
