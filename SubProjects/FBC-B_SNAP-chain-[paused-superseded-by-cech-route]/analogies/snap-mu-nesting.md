# Analogy: cancelling a `Localization.Monoidal.μ` pair whose object args are defeq-but-differently-nested

## Mode

## Slug

## Iteration

## Question
Is there a Mathlib-aligned resolution to the `Localization.Monoidal.μ` object-argument
`⋙`-nesting representation mismatch blocking `tensorObjAssoc_eq_localizedAssociator`
(SectionGradedRing.lean:1796)? Specifically: (1) a coherence/normal-form/μ-naturality lemma
that cancels two defeq-but-differently-nested μ WITHOUT restating the keystones; if not,
(2) is the keystone-restatement fix idiomatic, or is there a cleaner route?

## Project artifact(s)
- `SectionGradedRing.lean:1462` — `tensorObjLocalizedIso F G := μ⁻¹_{F♭,G♭} ≫ (c_F ⊗ c_G)`;
  its μ carries object args `(toPresheafOfModules X).obj F`, `… G` (F,G : X.Modules, **folded**).
- `SectionGradedRing.lean:1727/1751` — keystones `sheafification_whisker{Right,Left}_unit_eq_mu`;
  their final μ.hom carries the inferred arg `(toPresheafOfModules X).obj (sheafification.obj P)`,
  but Lean elaborates it from the adjunction-unit codomain as the **fused** `(sheafification ⋙
  SheafOfModules.forget ⋙ restrictScalars).obj P`.
- `SectionGradedRing.lean:1862` — the `sorry`: `Iso.hom_inv_id_assoc` cannot fire to cancel the
  μ-pair because the two μ isos are not syntactically identical.

## Root cause (precise)
`Localization.Monoidal.μ (X Y : C)` (Mathlib `Localization/Monoidal/Basic.lean:184`,
sig `(L').obj X ⊗ (L').obj Y ≅ (L').obj (X ⊗ Y)`) is keyed on its **source-category objects
`X Y : C`**. The two μ occurrences that must cancel carry the *same mathematical* `C`-object but
in two representations separated by a STACK of three rfl-defeq folds:
1. `Functor.comp_obj`: `(F ⋙ G).obj x`  vs  `G.obj (F.obj x)` (fused vs split).
2. opaque def `toPresheafOfModules X := SheafOfModules.forget _`
   (`Mathlib/AlgebraicGeometry/Modules/Sheaf.lean:59`) — simp won't unfold it.
3. opaque def `tensorObj A B := sheafification.obj (a ⊗ b)` (`SectionGradedRing.lean:94`) —
   `tensorObjLocalizedIso` keeps it FOLDED; any keystone applied at `P = a⊗b` produces it UNFOLDED.

`rw`/`simp`/`erw`/`change`-via-`slice`/`rfl`-bridge fail on the *full* goal: `rw`/`simp` match
keyed up to reducible transparency (won't cross the opaque defs); full-goal `change`/`erw` force
`whnf` over a giant term ⇒ timeout. The defeq is real (a `… := rfl` bridge typechecks) — the
problem is purely *syntactic unification at scale*.

## Decisions identified

### Decision 1: Is there a zero-restate Mathlib coherence that cancels the μ-pair?
- **Mathlib idiom**: NONE applies as-is.
  - `μ_natural_left` / `μ_natural_right` (`Basic.lean:188/200`, both `@[reassoc (attr := simp)]`;
    inv variants `:194/:206`; reassoc forms `μ_natural_left_assoc` used at `:361`) slide a μ past
    `(L').map f ▷ (L').obj Y` — but they REQUIRE the object args to already be the *same* `C`-terms.
    They do not bridge a representational mismatch.
  - `Functor.comp_obj` / `Functor.comp_map` ARE the `@[simp]` normalizers (generated by
    `@[simps (attr := grind =) obj]` on `Functor.comp`, `Functor/Basic.lean:116`/`:124`; normal
    form = the SPLIT `G.obj (F.obj x)`). But they cannot reconcile here: the competing form hides
    the composition behind opaque `toPresheafOfModules`/`tensorObj`, which simp will not unfold.
  - `Iso.hom_inv_id_assoc` / `Iso.inv_hom_id_assoc` need the iso *syntactically identical*.
- **Gap**: divergent-with-cost. No `Localization.Monoidal` lemma cancels μ across representations.
- **Verdict**: NEEDS reconciliation of representation first (no free coherence).

### Decision 2: keystone-restatement (prover's proposed fix) — idiomatic / sufficient?
- **Prover's fix**: restate keystones so the final μ object arg is written canonically as
  `(toPresheafOfModules X).obj (sheafification.obj _)`, proven defeq-tolerantly.
- **Gap**: divergent-with-cost — RIGHT DIRECTION but INSUFFICIENT ALONE. Pinning the μ object arg
  removes fold #1 (`comp_obj`) but NOT fold #3: `tensorObjLocalizedIso` carries `(toPresheafOfModules
  X).obj (tensorObj A B)` with `tensorObj A B` FOLDED, whereas the keystone at `P = a⊗b` gives
  `… (sheafification.obj (a⊗b))` UNFOLDED. Syntactic identity (needed by `hom_inv_id_assoc`) still
  fails unless the *application site* also folds `sheafification.obj (a⊗b)` back to `tensorObj A B`.
- **Verdict**: PROCEED-with-augmentation (must combine with Decision 3's isolation + `show`).

### Decision 3: the robust idiom — isolate + `show`/`change` to pin ONE representation
- **Mathlib idiom**: when two terms are defeq but not syntactically equal, cancellation succeeds by
  (a) shrinking the goal so `whnf` is cheap, then (b) using `show`/`change` (which run full-
  transparency `isDefEq`, unlike keyed `rw`) to rewrite BOTH μ's to one chosen literal form,
  making them identical so `Iso.hom_inv_id_assoc` fires. Equivalently `set μ_AB := … with h` to give
  both occurrences one local name *after* `show` aligns them.
- This is exactly the effort-break: `hK_lhs : <lhs> = K`, `hK_rhs : <rhs> = K`, assembly =
  `hK_lhs.trans hK_rhs.symm`. The full-goal failure is a *scale* artifact; on each small `hK_*`,
  `show`/`slice`+`erw` terminate (matches the iter-010 Knowledge-Base idiom: `slice`+`erw`
  discharges the `tensorObj`/`sheafification.obj` junction defeq that defeats plain `rw`/`simp`).
- **Verdict**: ALIGN_WITH_MATHLIB (this is the standard defeq-at-scale escape hatch).

### Decision 4 (deeper): the RHS μ-juggling re-derives `associator_hom_app`
- **Mathlib idiom**: `Localization.Monoidal.associator_hom_app` (`Basic.lean:234`) already packages
  `(α_ (L'.obj X₁) (L'.obj X₂) (L'.obj X₃)).hom = ((μ _ _).hom ⊗ₘ 𝟙) ≫ (μ _ _).hom ≫ L'.map (α_
  X₁X₂X₃).hom ≫ (μ _ _).inv ≫ (𝟙 ⊗ₘ (μ _ _).inv)`. Its object args are inferred `_`, so the μ's
  unify NATIVELY (no nesting mismatch) — and Mathlib's own `MonoidalCategory` `associativity`
  field is discharged by `simp [associator_hom_app L W ε X Y Z]` (`Basic.lean:469`).
- **Project's path**: hand-derives this same decomposition via the four seam lemmas + manual
  μ-cancellation, which is precisely what re-introduces the second raw-μ representation that
  collides with `tensorObjLocalizedIso`'s μ.
- **Gap**: divergent-with-cost. Routing the *localized* side of `tensorObjAssoc_eq_localizedAssociator`
  through `associator_hom_app` (then conjugating by counits) collapses the bespoke μ-chase to the
  counit-coherence only and sidesteps the nesting collision at its source.
- **Verdict**: ALIGN_WITH_MATHLIB (use `associator_hom_app`; do not re-derive it).

## Recommendation
Two complementary moves, both Mathlib-grounded:

1. **Cancellation mechanism (unblocks the current shape):** effort-break into standalone
   `hK_lhs`/`hK_rhs` lemmas (assembly = `hK_lhs.trans hK_rhs.symm`). Inside each, after applying the
   keystones, **pin both μ's to one literal form with `show`/`change`** (full-transparency defeq,
   succeeds where keyed `rw` fails) — choose `tensorObjLocalizedIso`'s form
   `Localization.Monoidal.μ … ((toPresheafOfModules X).obj (tensorObj A B)) ((toPresheafOfModules
   X).obj C)` — then `rw [Iso.hom_inv_id_assoc]` (or `Iso.inv_hom_id_assoc`) fires. Augment with the
   keystone-restatement (pin the keystone's own final μ arg explicitly, killing fold #1 up front so
   `show` only has to bridge fold #3). `show` was failing ONLY because it ran on the giant goal;
   isolation makes its `whnf` cheap.

2. **Structural alignment (kills the collision at the source):** rewrite the localized side via
   `Localization.Monoidal.associator_hom_app` (`Basic.lean:234`) instead of hand-assembling it from
   seams — its inferred-`_` μ object args unify natively, and Mathlib discharges the analogous
   coherence with one `simp [associator_hom_app …]`. This is the lower-fragility route for future
   iters even if move #1 lands the proof now.
