0.1 Peer dependency anchors

This chapter records reusable infrastructure already present in peer DAGs. The blocks here are dependency anchors for the later formal work; they are not mathematical claims of Sawin’s paper. They cover the classical side of the §5 application (the geometry of a hyperelliptic Jacobian, its theta loci, symmetric powers, canonical differentials, and pushforwards of sheaves). The genuinely new layer of the paper — singular support, characteristic cycles in characteristic \(p\), nearby/vanishing cycles, and intersection theory in the cotangent bundle — has no peer anchor and is introduced from scratch in the Sawin_Terminology chapter.

0.2 Terminology: transversality, singular support, characteristic cycle

This chapter fixes the notation and the basic objects of Beilinson’s and Saito’s theory of singular support and characteristic cycles in characteristic \(p\), following §1 and §2 of the paper. All schemes are smooth over a perfect field \(k\); \(\ell \) is a prime invertible in \(k\).

Source: Sawin, §1, Definition (projectivization). #

Uses

Lean declarations

  • MR4228499.polarMultiplicity

Source: Sawin, §1, Definition polar-multiplicity. Let \(X\) be smooth of dimension \(n\), \(C\) a conical cycle on \(T^*X\) of dimension \(n\), and \(x\in X\). For \(0\le i{\lt}n\), let \(V\) be a rank-\((i+1)\) sub-bundle of \(T^*X\) near \(x\) with \(V_x\) a general point of the Grassmannian of \((i+1)\)-planes in \((T^*X)_x\). 0.3 The characteristic-\(p\) Massey bound

This chapter proves the main theorem: the stalk dimension \(\dim \mathcal{H}^{-i}(K)_x\) of a perverse \(\mathbb {F}_\ell \)-sheaf is bounded by the \(i\)th polar multiplicity of \(\mathit{CC}(K)\) at \(x\) (Theorem 29). §3 establishes an alternate, manifestly well-defined form of the polar multiplicity; §4 carries out the pencil-of-conics induction.

Lemma 21 Independence of the polar intersection from the choice of \((Y,V)\)
#

Uses

Lean declarations

  • MR4228499.intersection_comparison

Source: Sawin, §3, Lemma intersection-comparison. Let \(X\) be smooth, \(C\) a conical cycle on \(T^*X\) of dimension \(\dim X\), \(x\in X\), and \(0\le i{\lt}\dim X\). For \(Y\subset X\) smooth of dimension \(\dim X-i\) through \(x\) and \(V\) a rank-\((i+1)\) sub-bundle of \(T^*X\) on \(Y\) whose strict transforms of \(\mathbb {P}(C),\mathbb {P}(V)\) do not meet in the exceptional divisor of the blowup of \(\mathbb {P}(T^*X)\) at the fibre over \(x\), the contribution of the fibre over \(x\) to \(\mathbb {P}(C)\cap \mathbb {P}(V)\) depends only on \(i\). A general pair \(((TY)_x,V_x)\) satisfies the hypothesis, so such \((Y,V)\) exist.

Proof ▶

Work locally and deform \((Y,V)\) to \((Y',V')\) through a connected family of local complete intersections (convex combination of defining equations). The emptiness of the strict-transform intersection in the exceptional divisor is open, so the whole family can be taken to satisfy it; the intersection locus is closed and disjoint from \(x\), hence avoids a neighborhood of \(x\), so the contribution at \(x\) is constant in the family. For genericity: \(\mathbb {P}(C)\) has dimension \(\dim X-1\), the exceptional fibre \(\mathbb {P}((TX)_x)\times \mathbb {P}((T^*X)_x)\) has dimension \(2\dim X-2\), and the strict transform of \(\mathbb {P}(V)\) is \(\mathbb {P}((TY)_x)\times \mathbb {P}(V_x)\) of dimension \(\dim X-1\); for general subspaces the intersection has expected dimension \(-1\), i.e. is empty.

  • MR4228499.nearby_formula

Source: Sawin, §4, Lemma nearby-formula. Let \(X\) be smooth, \(Y\) a smooth curve, \(K\in D^b_c(X,\mathbb {F}_\ell )\), and let \(\mathit{CC}'(K)\), \(\mathit{SS}'(K)\) denote \(\mathit{CC}(K)\), \(\mathit{SS}(K)\) with the cotangent space at \(x\) removed. If \(f:X\to Y\) is smooth projective, \(\mathit{SS}'(K)\)-transversal, and the fibres of \(\mathit{SS}'(K)\) over \(Y\) have dimension \(\dim X-1\), then for \(Z=f^{-1}(f(x))\) with inclusion \(i\),

\[ i^!\mathit{CC}'(K) = \mathit{CC}(R\Psi _f K). \]
Proof ▶

Away from \(x\), \(\mathit{SS}'(K)\)-transversality makes \(f\) locally acyclic, so \(R\Phi _f K=0\) and \(R\Psi _f K=i^*K\); by Saito Theorem 7.6 and Lemma 24, \(\mathit{CC}(R\Psi _f K)=i^!\mathit{CC}'(K)\) there. Hence the difference \(i^!\mathit{CC}'(K)-\mathit{CC}(R\Psi _f K)\) is a multiple of the cotangent space at \(x\); pairing with the zero-section and using the index formula (Saito Theorem 7.13) \((\mathit{CC}(R\Psi _f K),Z)=\chi (f^{-1}(\eta ),K)\) on one side, and projection-formula manipulations \(i_*(di)^*Z = N^*Z\) (Fulton 8.1.1(c)) together with the constancy of \((\mathit{CC}'(K),N^*f^{-1}(y'))\) in a smooth family on the other, shows the two pairings agree, forcing the multiple to be zero.

Lemma 26 A general pencil of conics is transversal

Lean declarations

  • MR4228499.genericity_lemma

Source: Sawin, §4, Lemma genericity-lemma. Let \(X\subseteq \mathbb {P}^n\) be smooth, \(C\subseteq T^*X\) closed conical of dimension \(\dim X\), and \(x\in X\) a point whose cotangent space is not contained in \(C\). For a general pencil of conic sections \(\overline X\subseteq X\times \mathbb {P}^1\) with projections \(p:\overline X\to X\), \(q:\overline X\to \mathbb {P}^1\): \(p\) is properly \(C\)-transversal, \(q\) is \(p^\circ C\)-transversal near the conic through \(x\), and the fibres of \(p^\circ C\) over \(\mathbb {P}^1\) are \((\dim X-1)\)-dimensional near that conic.

Proof ▶

The base locus \(Y\) of a generic pencil is smooth of codimension \(2\) (Bertini), and \(p\) is the blow-up of \(X\) along \(Y\). Counting conditions on the pencil: for \((y,\omega )\in C\), “\(y\in Y\)” is codimension \(2\) and “\(\omega \) transverse to \(Y\)” is codimension \(\dim X-2\), while the space of such \((y,\omega )\) up to scaling is \((\dim X-1)\)-dimensional, so the bad locus is codimension \(1\) — non-generic — giving \(C\)-transversality; the dimension count of \(p^*C\) gives proper transversality. For \(q\): \(p^\circ C\)-transversality is open, and one checks \(dq(\omega _0)\notin p^\circ C\) pointwise on the conic through \(x\) via analogous codimension counts, using that the cotangent space at \(x\) is not in \(C\). Finally each component of \(p^\circ C\) has dimension \(\dim X\), so its fibres over \(\mathbb {P}^1\) have dimension \(\dim X-1\) once the finitely many points forced into a single conic are excluded from the neighborhood.

Source: Sawin, §4, Lemma final-nearby-formula. With \(\overline X\), \(p\), \(q\) a general pencil of conics, \(\overline x=p^{-1}(x)\), \(y=q(\overline x)\), and \(i:q^{-1}(y)\hookrightarrow \overline X\): (1) \(\mathit{CC}(R\Psi _q p^*K)=i^!p^!\mathit{CC}'(K)\); (2) \(R\Phi _q p^*K\) is supported at \(x\); (3) \(-\operatorname {dimtot}(R\Phi _q p^*K)_x\) equals the multiplicity of the cotangent space at \(x\) in \(\mathit{CC}(K)\); (4) if \(K\) is perverse, \((R\Phi _q p^*K)_x\) is supported in degree \(-1\).

Proof ▶

(1) By Lemma 26, \(p\) is properly \(\mathit{SS}(K)\)-transversal and étale at \(x\), so \(\mathit{CC}(p^*K)=p^!\mathit{CC}(K)\) (Saito Theorem 6.6); apply Lemma 25 to \(p^*K\) and \(q\). (2) \(q\) is \(\mathit{SS}(p^*K)\)-transversal near \(x\) off \(x\), so \(p^*K\) is locally \(q\)-acyclic there and \(R\Phi _q p^*K\) is supported at \(x\). (3) \(x\) is at most isolated characteristic, so \(-\operatorname {dimtot}(R\Phi _q p^*K)_x = (\mathit{CC}(p^*K),(dq)^*\omega )_x\); the only component of \(\mathit{SS}(p^*K)\) meeting \((dq)^*\omega \) is \(N^*x\), with intersection \(1\), giving the multiplicity of \(N^*x\) in \(\mathit{CC}(p^*K)=\mathit{CC}(K)\). (4) \(p^*K[-1]\) is perverse near \(x\) on the generic fibre, so by Gabber’s theorem (Illusie, Corollary 4.6) \(R\Phi _q(p^*K)[-1]\) is perverse; being supported at a point it sits in degree \(0\), so the unshifted complex is in degree \(-1\).

  • MR4228499.first_Massey

Source: Sawin, §4, Theorem first-Massey. Let \(X\) be a smooth variety over a perfect field \(k\), \(\ell \) invertible in \(k\), and \(K\) a perverse sheaf of \(\mathbb {F}_\ell \)-modules on \(X\). Then for every \(x\in X\),

\[ \dim _{\mathbb {F}_\ell }\mathcal{H}^{-i}(K)_x \ \le \ \gamma ^i_{\mathit{CC}(K)}(x). \]
Proof ▶

Étale-locally we may take \(X\subseteq \mathbb {P}^n\) smooth projective. Induct on \(i\). From the distinguished triangle \(p^*K\to R\Psi _q p^*K\to R\Phi _q p^*K\) take stalk cohomology at \(x\). Since \(R\Psi _q p^*K[-1]\) is perverse, \(\mathcal{H}^0(R\Psi _q p^*K)_x=0\), so for \(i=0\), \(\dim \mathcal{H}^0(K)_x\le \dim (R\Phi _q p^*K)^{-1}_x\le -\operatorname {dimtot}(R\Phi _q p^*K)_x\), the multiplicity of the cotangent space at \(x\) in \(\mathit{CC}(K)\), which by Lemma 28 is \(\gamma ^0_{\mathit{CC}(K)}(x)\). For \(i{\gt}0\), Lemma 27(4) makes the connecting map vanish, so \(\dim \mathcal{H}^{-i}(K)_x\le \dim \mathcal{H}^{-i}(R\Psi _q p^*K)_x\); the induction hypothesis bounds this by the \((i-1)\)st polar multiplicity of \(\mathit{CC}(R\Psi _q p^*K[-1])\), which by Lemma 27(1) and Lemma 28 equals \(\gamma ^i_{\mathit{CC}(K)}(x)\).

0.4 Application to the conjecture of Shende and Tsimerman

This chapter applies the Massey bound to bound the Betti numbers of intersections of translates of theta loci in a hyperelliptic Jacobian (Theorem 38), recovering and tightening the characteristic-zero computation of Shende and Tsimerman, and deduces the equidistribution statement. Tsimerman’s appendix supplies the multiplicity bound (Theorem 41) that controls the polar multiplicities.

Throughout, \(C\) is a smooth projective hyperelliptic curve of genus \(g\) over an algebraically closed field \(k\) of characteristic \(\ne 2\), with hyperelliptic involution \(\tau \), Jacobian \(J\), and symmetric powers \(C^{(n)}\). A fixed half-hyperelliptic divisor identifies all degree-\(n\) divisor-class spaces with \(J\), so \([P+\tau (P)]=0\).
