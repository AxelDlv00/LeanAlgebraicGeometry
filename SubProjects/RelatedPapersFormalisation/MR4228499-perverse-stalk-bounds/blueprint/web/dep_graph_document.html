Definition 1Jacobian of a curve anchor

The peer project Algebraic-Jacobian-Challenge, in its chapter on the Jacobian, supplies the Jacobian \(J\) of a smooth projective curve \(C\) as a group object representing degree-zero divisor classes. Sawin’s §5 works on the Jacobian of a hyperelliptic curve, identifying each component \(J^{(n)}\) of degree-\(n\) classes with \(J\) after fixing a base point, and uses the trivial cotangent bundle \(T^*J \cong J \times H^0(C,K_C)\). This anchor is that group object.

Definition 2Weil divisors and symmetric powers anchor

The peer project Algebraic-Jacobian-Challenge, in its chapter on Weil divisors, supplies effective divisors on a curve and (via prime divisors and their multiplicities) the symmetric power \(C^{(n)} = \operatorname {Sym}^n C\) as a moduli space of degree-\(n\) effective divisors, together with the Abel–Jacobi map \(\pi _n : C^{(n)} \to J\) sending a divisor to its class. Definition 19Characteristic cycle \(CC(K)\)

Source: Sawin, §2, Definition (Saito 5.10). \(\mathit{CC}(K)\) is the unique \(\mathbb {Z}\)-linear combination of irreducible components of \(\mathit{SS}(K)\) such that for every étale \(j:W\to X\), every \(f:W\to Y\) to a smooth curve, and every at most isolated \(h^\circ \mathit{SS}(\mathcal{F})\)-characteristic point \(u\in W\) of \(f\),

\[ -\operatorname {dimtot}\bigl(R\Phi _f(j^*K)\bigr)_u = (j^*\mathit{CC}(K),(df)^*\omega )_{T^*W,u}, \]

for \(\omega \) a meromorphic one-form without zero or pole at \(f(u)\). Existence and uniqueness are Saito Theorem 5.9; integrality (Beilinson–Deligne) is Saito Theorem 5.18. By Saito Proposition 5.14(2), \(\mathit{SS}(K) = \operatorname {supp}\mathit{CC}(K)\).

Definition 7Polar multiplicity, projective form

Source: Sawin, §1, Definition polar-multiplicity. Let \(X\) be smooth of dimension \(n\), \(C\) a conical cycle on \(T^*X\) of dimension \(n\), and \(x\in X\). For \(0\le i{\lt}n\), let \(V\) be a rank-\((i+1)\) sub-bundle of \(T^*X\) near \(x\) with \(V_x\) a general point of the Grassmannian of \((i+1)\)-planes in \((T^*X)_x\).
